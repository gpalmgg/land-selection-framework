# Track 3 — Regenerative-Community Activity & Verified Network Counts

Research date: 2026-07-03. Primary directories used: GEN (ecovillage.org / gen-europe.org / gen-us.net), FIC (ic.org), Red Ibérica de Ecoaldeas (ecoaldeas.org), Permaculture Association (permaculture.org.uk), Colibris/Oasis (colibris-lafabrique.org).

## How to read this document

- The site's "regenerative-network density" number is a **count of named ecovillages + permaculture/demonstration sites within ~100 km** of each region's centroid.
- **No public directory returns a clean radius count.** GEN and FIC list by country/state, not by 100 km buffer; the RIE lists by named member only. So every "recommended number" below is a **defensible estimate built from named, sourced sites**, and is flagged FIRM (anchor site directory-verified) or SOFT (assembled from scattered sources, no single directory).
- **Do not inflate.** Where a region is genuinely thin, it is marked THIN and the honest number is low. That is more useful to a reciprocity-first tool than a padded count.

## Global baselines (for context)

- GEN interactive map: **1,251 ecovillage communities** mapped worldwide (GEN International 2025 Annual Report; the 2024 report cited a slightly lower figure).
- FIC / ic.org Communities Directory: **1,200+ intentional communities**, strongest coverage in the USA.
- These two directories overlap heavily but neither is exhaustive; many real projects are in neither.

## CORRECTIONS TO KNOWN ERRORS (read first)

1. **Cloughjordan Ecovillage is in Co. Tipperary — NOT West Cork or Connemara.** Confirmed: it sits in the townland of Oxpark beside Cloughjordan village, NW Tipperary near the Offaly border (67 acres, first residents Dec 2009). It is ~150 km from West Cork and must not be counted in the West Cork/Connemara region. The genuinely in-region West Cork site is **The Hollies, Enniskeane** (co-founded 1998; notably one of its founders was Rob Hopkins, later of the Transition movement). Connemara itself has **no anchor ecovillage** in GEN — that half of the region is THIN.

2. **Red Ibérica de Ecoaldeas lists ZERO members in Galicia and ZERO in Asturias.** Verified against the RIE members page (ecoaldeas.org/miembros-de-la-rie/) — the 11 current members are: Aldea de Olla (Valencia), Arterra Bizimodu (Navarra), Calabacino/Alájar (Huelva), Cortijo los Baños (Almería), IKI-GAIA (Málaga), Lakabe (Navarra), Los Portales (Sevilla), Matavenero (León), Molino de Guadalmesí (Cádiz), Valdepiélagos (Madrid), Vidàlia (Barcelona). The nearest to Galicia is **Matavenero (León)**. So Galician and Asturian counts must come from GEN and named-project sourcing, NOT the RIE — and both are correspondingly SOFT.

3. **ZEGG is in Brandenburg (Bad Belzig), NOT Saxony-Anhalt.** Only **Sieben Linden** (Beetzendorf, Altmark district of Salzwedel) is in Saxony-Anhalt. ZEGG is close to the state border but should not be attributed to Saxony-Anhalt. The Saxony-Anhalt anchor is Sieben Linden alone (ZEGG can be counted as "within ~100 km / adjacent" but flagged as Brandenburg).

4. **Huehuecoyotl is in Tepoztlán, Morelos — NOT Oaxaca.** Mexico's oldest ecovillage (1982) is ~90 min south of Mexico City in Morelos, roughly 400 km from Oaxaca. It cannot anchor the Oaxaca region. Oaxaca's regenerative density is real but sits in a **different register** — Indigenous *comunalidad*, communally held Zapotec/Mixtec/Chatino land, and agroecology networks — not Western-style intentional-community ecovillages. See the Oaxaca entry.

5. **O.U.R. Ecovillage is NOT in the Kootenays.** It is at Shawnigan Lake in the Cowichan Valley, Vancouver Island — several hundred km from the Kootenays. The Kootenays anchors are **Yasodhara Ashram** (Kootenay Bay, 1963) and the **Argenta** Quaker community, plus the region's Doukhobor communal legacy.

6. **Terra Perma is in the Laurentides (Harrington), NOT the Eastern Townships.** The Québec Eastern Townships / Cantons-de-l'Est region is THIN on formal ecovillages; Terra Perma should not be attributed to it.

## NAMED SITES & NOTES BY REGION

### Europe

**Alentejo (PT) — anchor: Tamera.** Tamera (Monte do Cerro, Odemira municipality, founded 1995; ~150 residents + wider cooperating circle) is one of Europe's most-cited ecovillages and dominates the region's density. GEN's Portugal country page is weighted to central Portugal (Guarda Rios nr Viseu, Awakened Life Project nr Benfeita, Future Eco CoHousing between Ericeira & Caldas), so Alentejo reads thin below Tamera. Realistic in-region: Tamera plus a handful of quinta-scale permaculture/regen smallholdings. Recommended **3**.

**Galicia (ES) — THIN / SOFT.** RIE = 0 members. GEN Galician-language listings are sparse. The dominant regen story here is **repoblación** rather than intentional community (see below): ~2,000-4,000 abandoned villages, an emerging Xunta digital catalogue of *aldeas modelo*, and agency-brokered village sales (Galician Country Homes). If the dataset wants an ecovillage count it is low (~2, soft); if it wants "regenerative activity," the repoblación frame is the honest and richer answer. Flag this to Gustaf — Galicia is a case where "network density" undersells the actual movement.

**Transylvania (RO) — anchors: Aurora, Kászon.** Aurora Community (SW Transylvania, founded Feb 2013, eco-spiritual + permaculture learning centre) and the Kászon/Kászonszék sustainable-community cluster in the Hungarian-speaking Székely Land. GEN Romania notes a "fast-growing" movement and hosted a Community Convergence at Aurora. Recommended **4**.

**West Cork & Connemara (IE) — anchor: The Hollies (West Cork only).** The Hollies Centre for Practical Sustainability, Enniskeane (24 acres, land bought 1998; cob-building revival hub). **Connemara has no GEN anchor** — treat the Connemara half as THIN. Do NOT list Cloughjordan here. Recommended **2**.

**Pembrokeshire (Wales) — anchor: Lammas.** Lammas / Tir-y-Gafel (Glandwr nr Crymych; first UK ecovillage to win prospective planning permission, 2009; 9 households + hub on 76 acres). Its legal precedent seeded Wales's national **One Planet Development** policy, under which numerous individual low-impact smallholdings now exist across Pembrokeshire (each an OPD dwelling), plus the West Wales LAND learning centre in the Permaculture Association network. Counting OPD smallholdings + LAND sites, ~**6** is defensible.

**Cévennes (FR) — cluster, no single giant.** Dense écolieu/oasis fabric via the Colibris "Oasis" network and Coopérative Oasis: Etika Mondo (Cévennes National Park), Oasis Les Coudriers (2016), Les Masades (Gard/Lozère/Ardèche border), Carmoune, plus the former CARAPA eco-hamlet (ended 2012). **Terre & Humanisme** (Pierre Rabhi's agroecology centre) is at Lablachère in the Ardèche — adjacent, arguably within ~100 km of the northern Cévennes. This is one of France's densest alternative-living areas; ~**9** and honestly could be higher.

**South Tirol (IT) — THIN.** No major ecovillage in Alto Adige proper. Italy's anchors are elsewhere: Damanhur (Piedmont), Torri Superiore (Liguria), Zonca (Italian Alps but not S. Tirol). South Tirol's regen activity is more in the register of organic/biodynamic maso farms and the Vinschgau agroecology scene than intentional communities. Recommended **2**, flagged thin.

**Asturias (ES) — SOFT, no directory.** RIE = 0. Named projects from regional listings: Savia Ecoaldea Vegana (western Asturias; veganism + agroecology + communal housing), El Mandala (Las Regueras; permaculture-ethics project), Vega de Orandi (Los Oscos). None are RIE members, so this is assembled sourcing, not a directory count. Recommended **4**. Repoblación applies here too (Asturias abandoned-village revival).

**Saxony-Anhalt (DE) — anchor: Sieben Linden.** Sieben Linden (Beetzendorf, Altmark; founded 1997; ~100 adults + 40 children; straw-bale + 100+ ha, University of Kassel measured its CO2 footprint at <1/3 German average). This is the region's clear anchor. ZEGG (Bad Belzig) is **Brandenburg**, not Saxony-Anhalt — list it only as an adjacent/near-region site with the correct state. Recommended **3**.

**Rural Estonia — anchor: Lilleoru.** Lilleoru (Valley of Flowers, ~25 km S of Tallinn, founded 1993; hosted GEN Europe 2018, ~600 attendees). GEN-Estonia (est. 2008) also lists Small Footprint (2014), Hobukooli Park (2006), Hundiallika Centre. Recommended **5**.

### North America

**Cascadia / Willamette Valley (OR) — anchor: Lost Valley.** Lost Valley Education Center & Ecovillage (Dexter), Alpha Farm (Deadwood, 1972 — actually Coast Range, ~55 mi NW of Eugene), Breitenbush Hot Springs worker co-op (Cascades), plus Eugene-area intentional communities and Columbia Ecovillage (Portland, ~2 hr N). Oregon is directory-dense. Recommended **10**.

**Vermont — anchor: Cobb Hill.** Cobb Hill Cohousing (Hartland, Donella Meadows's vision, 1995; 23 households on 270+ acres w/ working farm). A statewide directory cited ~**26** established/forming intentional communities in VT (Seven Days). Within ~100 km of central VT this easily supports **12**.

**Southern Appalachians / Asheville (NC) — anchors: Earthaven + Celo.** Earthaven (Black Mountain, 1994; ~75-100 residents; off-grid, micro-hydro + solar; partnered with School of Integrated Living). Celo Community (Burnsville, Yancey Co., **1937** — one of the oldest continuous land-trust intentional communities in the USA). Plus Asheville-area cohousing and a thick permaculture scene. NOTE: Common Ground Ecovillage and Hart's Mill are in **central** NC (Chatham/Orange Co.), outside the ~100 km Asheville buffer — do not count them here. Recommended **10**.

**Driftless (WI) — cluster: Kickapoo Valley / Viroqua.** Dancing Waters Permaculture Co-op (Gays Mills, Crawford Co.; consensus-run), Dreamtime Village (West Lima). Plus the Viroqua/Kickapoo cooperative-farming hub, **Marbleseed** (formerly MOSES, the organic-farming nonprofit, Viroqua) and **Organic Valley** (La Farge; began as a 7-farmer co-op, now 1,800+ farms). The agriculture orgs aren't ecovillages but are legitimate regenerative-network nodes. Recommended **7**.

**Ozarks (AR) — THIN.** No established ecovillage in the Arkansas Ozarks (multiple *forming* projects on permies.com forums, none realized). The nearest anchor is **East Wind Community** in the **Missouri** Ozarks (Ozark Co., nr Tecumseh — a large egalitarian income-sharing community), which may fall within ~100 km of the AR line depending on centroid. Dancing Rabbit is NE Missouri, too far. Recommended **2**, flagged thin. Be honest: this is the weakest region in the set for formal community density.

**Northern New Mexico / Taos — anchors: Lama, Greater World Earthship.** Lama Foundation (1967, interfaith spiritual community, 17 mi N of Taos), Greater World Earthship Community (world's first Earthship subdivision, ~130 full-time residents, 634 acres). Plus Taos-area land-based projects. Recommended **4**. (Reciprocity note: Taos Pueblo — living Tiwa community — is the dominant Indigenous presence; relevant to the site's Land-standing frame.)

**Nova Scotia — anchor: Treehouse Village.** Treehouse Village Ecohousing (Bridgewater; Atlantic Canada's first cohousing, on unceded Mi'kma'ki). **Asitu'lɨsk** (formerly Windhorse Farm, Wentzell's Lake) was returned to the Mi'kmaq in a 2021 land-back initiative and is now an Indigenous learning centre — highly relevant to the site's reciprocity spine (a settler-founded land project that *gave the land back*). Tatamagouche has a Free School / transition scene. Recommended **3**.

**Kootenays (BC) — anchors: Yasodhara, Argenta.** Yasodhara Ashram (Kootenay Bay, 1963; 120 acres; one of North America's longest-running spiritual centres). Argenta (Quaker community + former Argenta Friends School). Chuckleberry Community Farm (West Kootenays, 23 acres). Plus the region's deep **Doukhobor** communal-village legacy (early 20th c.). NOTE: O.U.R. Ecovillage is Vancouver Island, not here. Recommended **5**.

**Québec Eastern Townships — THIN.** No major in-region ecovillage; Terra Perma is in the Laurentides (Harrington). Some cohabitat/cohousing and a francophone "Écovillages et Éco-Communautés (Québec)" network exists but is diffuse. Recommended **2**, flagged thin.

**Oaxaca — different register (agroecology + comunalidad).** Huehuecoyotl (1982) is in Morelos, NOT Oaxaca. Oaxaca's regenerative density is real but not "ecovillage"-shaped: it lives in **communally governed Indigenous land** (Zapotec/Mixtec/Chatino *comunalidad*, ~communal + ejido tenure over most of the state's territory) and **agroecology networks** — e.g., Puente a la Salud Comunitaria (amaranth/food-sovereignty, Oaxaca), Tierra del Sol permaculture (nr Oaxaca city), and campesino seed/maize networks. For a reciprocity-first tool this is the most important framing note in the whole document: **in Oaxaca the "regenerative community" already exists and is Indigenous** — the honest number of Western intentional communities is low (~3), and that low number is itself the story. Recommend surfacing the comunalidad frame in the region blurb rather than a bare count.

## NEW / SCALED SITES 2024–2026

Thin verified pickings in these specific 20 regions; most 2024–26 launches surfaced are outside them. Verified named ones:

- **Earthbound Ecovillage** (Småland, Sweden) — new-generation project, 73 ha forest/lake/farmland, sociocratic; explicitly "replicable rural regeneration." NOT in a target region but the clearest recent European launch. Source: GEN Europe directory.
- **Avnø Oasis** (Denmark) — aspiring ecovillage, housing co-op + folk high school + regenerative enterprises. Not in a target region.
- **Vidàlia / Cal Vidal** (Puig-reig, Barcelona) — intentional community in a former textile-mill town, housing co-op + 75-year *masoveria urbana* contract; now a RIE member. Catalonia, not a target region, but a notable model (adaptive reuse + long-lease tenure) worth citing for the framework.
- **Asitu'lɨsk** (Nova Scotia, 2021–22) — the Windhorse-Farm land-back to the Mi'kmaq is the most relevant recent development *inside* a target region.
- **Galicia/Asturias repoblación (2022–2025)** — ongoing, accelerating (see below); the Xunta *aldeas modelo* catalogue is the newest institutional layer.

No verified 2024–26 new-founded ecovillage was confirmed inside Alentejo, Galicia, Transylvania, West Cork, Cévennes, S. Tirol, the Ozarks, or the Eastern Townships. Absence of a confirmed launch is reported as absence, not padded.

## REPOBLACIÓN / VILLAGE-REVIVAL MOVEMENTS (Iberia)

This is a parallel movement to ecovillages and, in Galicia/Asturias, the *stronger* signal:

- **Scale (Galicia):** ~2,000 abandoned villages commonly cited (some sources say up to ~4,000 counting partial abandonment); ~800 catalogued. The **Xunta de Galicia** has proposed a **digital catalogue of *aldeas modelo*** to match owners and buyers, with a **15% tax deduction** for acquisition/rehabilitation of housing in model-village projects (non-rental).
- **Brokers/registries:** **Galician Country Homes** — real-estate agency specialised in selling whole abandoned villages across Galicia, Asturias and León. **Proyecto Arraigo** (proyectoarraigo.es) — national repopulation intermediary connecting rural municipalities with incoming residents.
- **Asturias:** active "Repoblación de pueblos abandonados en Asturias" networks; village-revival + neorural in-migration.
- **Portugal parallel — Aldeias do Xisto:** ADXTUR-led programme (since 2001) revitalising **27 slate villages across 16 municipalities** in central Portugal (Serra da Lousã 12, Zêzere 6, Serra do Açor 5, Tejo-Ocreza 4) over ~5,000 km², with 200+ private operators. Tourism-led rather than intentional-community-led, but a real institutional repovoamento registry. There is also a **Lousã smart/sustainable-village** revival project.

Recommendation: for Galicia and Asturias, the site should either (a) add a small "village-revival" qualitative note alongside the ecovillage count, or (b) accept a low ecovillage number and cite repoblación as the live regenerative movement. Do not conflate the two into an inflated single count.

## PERMACULTURE & DEMONSTRATION-FARM DENSITY (where a public directory exists)

- **Wales / Pembrokeshire:** Permaculture Association (Britain) **LAND network** — 80+ demonstration projects nationally, 100+ demonstration sites total; Wales runs **Paramaethu Cymru**; the **West Wales LAND learning centre** and "Hwb Dysgu'r Tir" land-skills centre are in/near Pembrokeshire. No clean Pembrokeshire-only count published — check the Association's interactive map for the current figure.
- **Cévennes / France:** **Colibris "Oasis"** network + **Coopérative Oasis** list dozens of écolieux in the Gard/Lozère/Ardèche; **Terre & Humanisme** (Lablachère, Ardèche) is a flagship agroecology training centre. France's densest cluster in this set.
- **Driftless / WI:** **Marbleseed** (ex-MOSES, Viroqua) — largest organic-farming nonprofit in the US Midwest; **Organic Valley** (La Farge) — 1,800+ farmer co-op. Both are agriculture-network nodes, not ecovillages, but legitimately raise the region's regenerative density.
- **North Carolina:** School of Integrated Living (Earthaven) + a thick Asheville-area permaculture teaching scene.
- **Estonia:** GEN-Estonia acts as the de facto national permaculture/ecovillage register.
- **Ireland:** Cloughjordan (Tipperary) + The Hollies both appear in the Permaculture Association's projects listing (relevant to the *national* count, not the West Cork radius).

## GAPS & OPEN QUESTIONS (flag to Gustaf)

1. **No directory returns a 100 km radius count.** Every number here is an assembled estimate. If the site claims precision, it will be wrong. Recommend labelling the figure as an estimate with "named anchor sites" shown, rather than a hard integer.
2. **Galicia and Oaxaca break the metric.** In both, "ecovillage density" is low but *regenerative activity* is high (repoblación in Galicia; Indigenous comunalidad + agroecology in Oaxaca). The metric under-counts exactly where the reciprocity-first framing is most important. This is a feature to surface, not a bug to hide.
3. **Thin regions are genuinely thin:** Ozarks (AR), Eastern Townships (QC), South Tirol (IT), Connemara (IE half). Don't pad these.
4. **The FIC and GEN maps overlap and both undercount.** Many real projects (esp. small, land-based, or non-anglophone) are in neither. Treat the numbers as floors, not totals.
5. **Reciprocity-relevant standouts** worth a callout in region copy: **Asitu'lɨsk** (Nova Scotia land-back to Mi'kmaq, 2021) and **Oaxaca comunalidad** — both are cases where the "community already there" is the point, which aligns directly with the site's stated stance.
