# Track 2 — Land-Tenure & Rural-Settlement Policy Shifts (2024–2026)

**Scope:** Statutory and regulatory changes affecting who may lawfully buy and settle rural land across the 20 Land Selection Framework regions, 2024–2026. Reciprocity-to-host-community lens: the question is not "can capital acquire this" but "can a new community lawfully arrive without displacing the community already there."

**Compiled:** 2026-07-03. Primary sources prioritized (legislature/government portals). Items I could not verify to primary source are flagged **[UNVERIFIED]** or **[PARTIAL]**.

**One-line reading of the period:** The door is closing on *speculative and non-resident* acquisition across nearly every region, and simultaneously opening — narrowly — for *resident, working, collectively-held, agriculture-committed* arrivals. The two movements are the same movement. Reciprocity-shaped entry (residence + agricultural use + collective tenure) is increasingly the *only* legal entry, not merely the ethical one.

## 1. Recent statutory changes affecting who may buy/settle rural land (2024–2026)

### Québec — Bill 86 (Loi visant à assurer la pérennité du territoire agricole et sa vitalité)
- **What changed:** Overhauls the *Act respecting the preservation of agricultural land and agricultural activities* (CQLR c. P-41.1) and the CPTAQ regime. Non-farmer buyers of agricultural-zone land must obtain CPTAQ authorization and demonstrate genuine agricultural (not speculative/financial) intent. Introduces a **moratorium retroactive to 5 December 2024** on purchases of agricultural land by investment funds and, under conditions, by legal persons not registered as agricultural operations. Adds a land-rights monitoring/registration mechanism and gives municipalities power to surtax land "suitable for cultivation but not farmed."
- **Status / effective date:** **Assented 25 March 2025.** Note: assent ≠ full coming-into-force; several operative provisions await regulation/proclamation. The 5 Dec 2024 fund-moratorium is retroactive. **[PARTIAL — confirm which sections are in force vs. awaiting decree before citing "in effect."]**
- **Reciprocity read:** Directly favors working-farmer and community-agricultural entry over capital; hostile to LLC/fund site-shopping. Green Zone (see §4) remains the hard gate.
- **Sources:** National Assembly Bill 86 page — https://www.assnat.qc.ca/en/travaux-parlementaires/projets-loi/projet-loi-86-43-1.html ; consolidated statute (LégisQuébec) — https://www.legisquebec.gouv.qc.ca/en/document/cs/p-41.1 ; McMillan LLP analysis — https://mcmillan.ca/insights/publications/acquisition-bans-taxes-and-more-an-introduction-to-farm-based-bill-86/

### Wisconsin — AB 218 (foreign ownership; amends Wis. Stat. §710.02) [Driftless]
- **What changed (proposed):** Slashes the non-resident-alien/foreign-corporation land cap from **640 acres to 50 acres**; broadens the definition of "foreign person"; shortens the forced-divestiture window from **4 years to 3**; adds a ban on foreign-adversary/foreign-person interests within 10 miles of a military installation.
- **Status:** **NOT law.** Introduced April 2025 (Rep. Clint Moses et al.); **passed the Assembly, pending in Senate committee** as of early 2026; Wisconsin's biennial session ends ~early March 2026. **[FLAG: verify whether it passed the Senate / was signed before the session closed — as of last confirmed reporting it had not.]** Existing §710.02 (640-acre cap) remains the operative law.
- **Reciprocity read:** Tightens the ceiling but does not touch resident/citizen community formation; a US-resident collective is unaffected.
- **Sources:** 2025 AB 218 bill page — https://docs.legis.wisconsin.gov/2025/proposals/ab218 ; bill text — https://docs.legis.wisconsin.gov/document/proposaltext/2025/REG/AB218 ; current §710.02 context — https://www.wisbar.org/NewsPublications/WisconsinLawyer/Pages/Article.aspx?Volume=98&Issue=9&ArticleID=31246

### Wales — TAN 6 / One Planet Development [Pembrokeshire]
- **What's current:** One Planet Development (OPD) policy remains live in Technical Advice Note 6 (TAN 6, "Planning for Sustainable Rural Communities," July 2010) + Planning Policy Wales. OPD permits genuinely low-impact dwellings in open countryside where a household achieves an initial ecological footprint of ≤2.4 global hectares/person (trajectory to 1.88 gha) via a binding Management Plan + Ecological Footprint Analysis. This is *the* single most community-settlement-friendly rural planning route in the 20 regions.
- **2024–2026 change:** **[UNVERIFIED]** I could not confirm any 2025–2026 amendment to TAN 6 itself, nor a Welsh national-planning-statement change touching OPD. Search surfaced no primary-source revision. Treat OPD as **unchanged and live** pending confirmation. (Note: the Irish rural/Gaeltacht draft below is a *different* jurisdiction — do not conflate.)
- **Sources:** TAN 6 (GOV.WALES) — https://www.gov.wales/technical-advice-note-tan-6-planning-sustainable-rural-communities ; OPD practice guidance PDF — https://www.gov.wales/sites/default/files/publications/2019-06/planning-permission-one-planet-developments-in-open-countryside.pdf ; One Planet Council — http://www.oneplanetcouncil.org.uk/planning-policy/

### Ireland — Draft Sustainable Rural & Gaeltacht Housing National Planning Statement [West Cork/Connemara]
- **What changed:** On **30 June 2026** the Government approved a *proposed policy direction* for a Draft Sustainable Rural and Gaeltacht Housing National Planning Statement — the most significant rural-housing update since the 2005 Guidelines. Moves to environmental assessment + public consultation; final statement targeted before end of 2026. Key elements: "local need" defined by residence within ~3–10 km and years lived locally; **Gaeltacht** applicants must reside within 3 km and have 10 years' local residence (relaxable to 5 for demonstrated Irish speakers, with standardized language-competency assessment); permits a **second one-off house** on one's land for downsizing; removes local-authority caps on ribbon development / number of homes per farm.
- **Status:** **DRAFT — not yet in force.** Policy direction agreed; consultation pending; final statement expected end-2026.
- **Reciprocity read:** Sharpens the *local/linguistic-belonging* gate — explicitly privileges people already rooted (and, in Gaeltacht, Irish-speaking). An incoming non-local community faces a *harder* local-need test; an Irish-speaking or long-resident household, an easier one. This is a reciprocity gate written into planning law.
- **Sources:** DHLGH draft NPS policy direction — https://www.gov.ie/en/department-of-housing-local-government-and-heritage/publications/sustainable-rural-and-gaeltacht-housing-draft-nps-proposed-policy-direction/ ; DHLGH press release — https://www.gov.ie/en/department-of-housing-local-government-and-heritage/press-releases/draft-national-planning-statement-to-transform-approach-to-rural-housing-delivering-greater-clarity-and-consistency/ ; RTÉ 30 Jun 2026 — https://www.rte.ie/news/2026/0630/1580960-cabinet-housing-guidelines/

### Vermont — Act 181 (2024); partial repeal 2026 [Vermont]
- **What changed (Act 181, 2024):** Overhaul of Act 250. Created a professional 5-member Land Use Review Board. Shifted triggers from project *size* to *location* via tiers: **Tier 1** (downtowns/growth centers w/ water+sewer) — Act 250 relaxed/eliminated to encourage housing; **Tier 3** (most ecologically sensitive areas) — Act 250 review automatic; **Tier 2** (the rural majority) — existing rules largely continue. Raised the rural housing review threshold from 6–10 units to **50 units**. Added a contested **"road rule"** triggering review for new roads >800 ft or road+driveway networks >2,000 ft.
- **2026 reversal:** After heavy rural backlash (the road rule would have caught a single family home on rural land), the **Vermont House voted 7 May 2026 to partially repeal** — scrapping the road rule and the Tier 3 measures. **[FLAG: verify final enactment — as of last reporting this was a House vote; confirm Senate concurrence + Governor before citing as law.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** Net-loosening for rural building (higher unit thresholds), but the fight shows Vermont's rural-property-rights politics are volatile; a community relying on the 50-unit threshold should track the repeal's final shape.
- **Sources:** Act 181 as enacted (PDF) — https://legislature.vermont.gov/Documents/2024/Docs/ACTS/ACT181/ACT181%20As%20Enacted.pdf ; Act250.vermont.gov framework — https://act250.vermont.gov/new-land-use-review-framework-act-181 ; VTDigger 7 May 2026 partial repeal — https://vtdigger.org/2026/05/07/vermont-house-votes-to-partially-repeal-act-181/

### Nova Scotia — Non-Resident Provincial Deed Transfer Tax (10%) [Nova Scotia]
- **What changed:** Non-resident Provincial Deed Transfer Tax **doubled from 5% to 10%**, effective for Agreements of Purchase and Sale signed **after 31 March 2025** (i.e., 1 April 2025). Applies to residential property (incl. residential vacant land) with ≤3 dwelling units, on the higher of price or assessed value, **on top of** municipal deed transfer tax. **Exemption:** a non-resident individual grantee who *moves to* Nova Scotia within 6 months of closing.
- **Status:** **In force since 1 April 2025** (Financial Measures Act).
- **Reciprocity read:** Explicitly penalizes arriving-without-belonging and rewards actual relocation — a fiscal reciprocity gate. A community intending to *live there* can qualify out via the 6-month residence exemption; a group buying to hold pays 10%.
- **Sources:** Government of Nova Scotia (official) — https://www.novascotia.ca/non-resident-provincial-deed-transfer-tax ; official guidelines PDF — https://novascotia.ca/finance/en/home/taxation/tax101/docs/Nova-Scotia-Provincial-Non-resident-Deed-Transfer-Tax-Guidelines.pdf ; CBC — https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/nova-scotia/deed-transfer-tax-financial-measures-act-1.7482661

### Nova Scotia — Provincial Nominee quota cuts (immigration channel) [Nova Scotia]
- **What changed:** Federal Immigration Levels Plan 2025–27 halved the national PNP target (110,000 → 55,000). Nova Scotia's 2025 combined allocation cut ~50% to **3,150** (NSNP 3,570→1,785; Atlantic Immigration Program 2,730→1,365). NS prioritized applicants already living/working in-province (healthcare NAICS 62, construction NAICS 23). **October 2025:** federal top-up of +559, to **3,709**.
- **Reciprocity read:** For *non-Canadian* prospective settlers, the immigration door to Nova Scotia narrowed materially in 2025 — the binding constraint may be immigration status, not land price.
- **Sources:** CIC News 2025 — https://www.cicnews.com/2025/07/nova-scotia-will-not-process-certain-applications-for-provincial-nomination-0758179.html ; CBC (Oct 2025 increase) — https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/nova-scotia/nova-scotia-immigrant-program-increase-minister-diab-9.6941232

### Romania — Law 17/2014 + Law 175/2020 (extra-muros farmland pre-emption) [Transylvania]
- **What's current:** Law 17/2014 governs pre-emption on sale of *extra-muros* (outside-built-area) agricultural land. **Law 175/2020** (in force **13 October 2020**) expanded pre-emption beneficiaries from 4 to **13 categories across 7 priority ranks** (co-owners, lessees, neighbors, young farmers, the Romanian State, etc.). **A foreign natural person cannot directly buy Romanian agricultural land.** If no pre-emptor buys, a third-party purchaser must have **domicile/HQ in Romania ≥5 years** and have carried out **agricultural activity ≥5 years** prior; legal entities must show ≥75% of last-5-years' income from agriculture. **80% windfall tax** on resale within 8 years of acquisition.
- **2024 change:** **Law 116/2024** (following GEO 104/2022) further amended Law 17/2014 — ongoing legislative churn. **[PARTIAL — I did not verify the precise substantive changes in Law 116/2024; confirm before citing specifics.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** One of Europe's strongest *rootedness* gates: direct farmland purchase is effectively closed to non-residents and requires 5 years' domicile + agricultural track record. The standard workaround is a **Romanian SRL** (see §2).
- **Sources:** Schoenherr analysis — https://www.schoenherr.eu/content/romania-new-provisions-on-the-sale-of-farmland-located-outside-buildable-areas ; CMS legal update — https://cms.law/en/rou/legal-updates/Romania-introduces-restrictions-on-sale-and-purchase-of-agricultural-land ; CERHA HEMPEL — https://www.cerhahempel.com/blog/real-estate-insider/amendments-regarding-the-legal-status-of-agricultural-land-in-romania-new-conditions-in-case-of-sale-of-agricultural-land-located-outside-a-built-up-area

### Estonia — Restrictions on Acquisition of Immovables Act (RAIA) [rural Estonia]
- **What's current:** Citizens of Estonia/EEA/OECD may acquire agricultural + forest land **without restriction**. **Third-country** nationals need **local-council authorization**, conditioned on ≥6 months' permanent residence in Estonia *or* ≥1 year operating as a sole proprietor in agricultural production. Non-EEA/non-UK buyers face additional bans in small islands (Saaremaa, Hiiumaa, Vormsi, Muhu) and eastern border municipalities (Narva, Narva-Jõesuu, Sillamäe), and a **10-hectare cap** on forest/agricultural plots.
- **2026 development:** **January 2026** Interior Ministry draft would bar **Russian and Belarusian nationals** without long-term residence permits (and certain corporate structures they ultimately beneficially own) from buying property. **[STATUS: draft only — not enacted at time of writing.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** For a US/UK/EU-origin community, Estonia is comparatively open (OECD carve-out covers the US); the binding gate is the third-country residence test only for non-OECD members.
- **Sources:** RAIA consolidated text (Riigi Teataja, official) — https://www.riigiteataja.ee/en/eli/ee/506102021003/consolide/current

## 2. Foreign-buyer / non-resident restrictions

### Canada — federal foreign-buyer ban (Prohibition on the Purchase of Residential Property by Non-Canadians Act) [Nova Scotia, Kootenays, Québec ET]
- **Status:** Extended **4 Feb 2024** from expiry 1 Jan 2025 to **1 Jan 2027**.
- **Rural / relevant exemptions:** Applies only to **Census Metropolitan Areas (CMAs) and Census Agglomerations (CAs)** — i.e., cities and larger towns. Property **outside CMAs/CAs is not covered.** **Vacant land** was removed from the ban's scope as of **27 March 2023** (non-Canadians may buy vacant residential/mixed-use land). Agricultural/rural parcels typically fall outside CMAs/CAs and outside the ban.
- **Reciprocity read:** The federal ban is **largely irrelevant to genuinely rural settlement** — Kootenays backcountry, rural Nova Scotia, and Eastern-Townships farmland mostly sit outside CMAs/CAs. The *binding* Canadian gates are provincial: NS deed-transfer tax, BC ALR, Québec CPTAQ/Bill 86, and immigration status.
- **Sources:** CMHC (official) — https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/professionals/housing-markets-data-and-research/housing-research/consultations/prohibition-purchase-residential-property-non-canadians-act

### United States — state-level foreign-farmland wave (2024–2026) [Cascadia, Driftless, Ozarks, So. Appalachians, Vermont, N. New Mexico]
- **The wave:** A sustained 2023–2025 surge of state laws restricting foreign (esp. "foreign adversary") ownership of agricultural land. Per the National Agricultural Law Center's 2025 recap, restrictions continued expanding across states.
- **Region-specific:**
  - **Arkansas [Ozarks]:** **Act 636 of 2023** (SB 383, signed 11 Apr 2023) bars "prohibited foreign parties" from acquiring agricultural land and certain foreign business entities from any real property; created an Office of Agricultural Intelligence; Arkansas became the **first state to enforce** (Oct 2023, ordered a Chinese-owned firm to divest, $280k fine). **2025 amendments via HB 1358 / HB 1680** reportedly broadened it. **[PARTIAL — 2025 amendment specifics unverified to primary source; confirm act numbers/effective dates before citing.]**
  - **North Carolina [So. Appalachians]:** **HB 463** would bar "adversarial foreign governments" from acquiring/leasing agricultural land and land within 25 miles of a military installation. **[STATUS: proposed/under consideration — verify enactment.]**
  - **Oregon [Cascadia]:** Existing law restricts foreign ownership of public lands/farmland; **new foreign-ownership legislation proposed in 2025.** **[STATUS: unverified — confirm whether any 2025 Oregon bill was enacted.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** These laws target *foreign-government/adversary* capital, not domestic community formation. A **US-person or US-resident collective** is essentially unaffected; a foreign-national founder should check the specific state's "prohibited foreign party" definition and any resident-alien carve-out.
- **Sources:** National Ag Law Center 2025 recap — https://nationalaglawcenter.org/2025-legislative-recap-continued-expansion-of-state-level-foreign-ownership-restrictions/ ; Arkansas Act 636 (2023) PDF — https://www.arkleg.state.ar.us/Home/FTPDocument?path=/ACTS/2023R/Public/ACT636.pdf ; CRS overview — https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/LSB11013

### EU member-state rules for non-EU buyers
- **Romania:** Direct farmland purchase closed to non-resident foreign individuals; standard route is a **Romanian SRL** (limited company) that itself must satisfy the residence/agriculture/75%-income tests to buy extra-muros land post-2020 — the SRL is not a clean bypass anymore. (See §1.)
- **Hungary** (comparator, not a target region): Under **Act CXXII of 2013**, third-country nationals, foreign states, and certain legal persons **cannot acquire agricultural land**; may lease up to 1,200 ha for ≤20 years. Even EU nationals must qualify as farmers (3 years' experience or ag degree). Ban on foreign farmland ownership extended.
- **Estonia:** OECD carve-out (covers US) — see §1.
- **Spain, Portugal, France, Italy, Germany:** No blanket non-EU purchase ban on rural land; the gates are farmland-protection/pre-emption regimes (§4) and, in Portugal, the **removal of the real-estate Golden Visa route (2023)** — property purchase **no longer grants residency**; qualifying investment is now funds/business/heritage (≥€250k–€500k). This does not bar purchase, but removes the residency incentive that drove non-EU rural buying in the Alentejo.
- **Sources:** Hungary Act CXXII/2013 (ELRA) — https://www.elra.eu/contact-point-contribution/hungary/6701/ ; UNCTAD (Hungary ban extension) — https://investmentpolicy.unctad.org/investment-policy-monitor/measures/2334/ ; Portugal Golden Visa reform — https://getgoldenvisa.com/portugal-golden-visa-program

## 3. Community land trusts & collective-tenure vehicles

The most important *reciprocal entry route* across regions: hold land collectively, remove it from the speculative market, lease long-term. 2024–2026 saw this sector formalize.

- **Europe — sector maturation:** A **2025 "Community Land Trusts in Europe: State of the Sector Report"** was published (European CLT Network / CLT Europe), signaling institutional coming-of-age. Key operating models relevant to target regions:
  - **Terre de Liens (France) [Cévennes]:** ~54,000 members, ~1,400 volunteers, ~10,000 ha held for agriculture. Buys farmland, leases to farmers on ecological terms — the leading model for lawful, reciprocal French farmland access that *works with* SAFER rather than against it.
  - **Terre-en-vue (Belgium):** three-structure model (nonprofit + shareholder cooperative + foundation) — a portable template.
  - **Kulturland (Germany) [Saxony-Anhalt]:** cooperative that had notarized purchases for 21 farms / ~308 ha nationwide.
  - **Ecological Land Cooperative (UK/Wales) [Pembrokeshire]:** pairs CSA + CLT structures; natural partner to OPD (§1).
- **United States — Agrarian Commons [all US regions]:** **Agrarian Trust** (national 501(c)(3)) launched the **Agrarian Commons** model **4 May 2020**, live in **10 states**. Local Agrarian Commons buy farmland (national fundraising support) and lease via long-term, equity-building leases (99-year where state law allows), centering BIPOC land access. This is the clearest US vehicle for a community to *hold land in common and lease reciprocally* rather than individually own.
- **Mexico — comunal/ejido [Oaxaca]:** ~80% of Oaxaca land is collectively managed across 1,602+ ejidos/comunidades. **Foreigners cannot be ejidatarios/comuneros** (membership reserved for Mexican nationals). Collective entry is by *assembly consent*, not purchase — the deepest reciprocity gate in the 20 regions (see §5).
- **Status of 2024–2026 change:** Sector-formalization (EU report, continued Agrarian Commons expansion) rather than statutory rewrite. **[UNVERIFIED: no single new CLT-enabling statute identified in target jurisdictions 2024–2026; the developments are institutional/financial, not legislative.]**
- **Sources:** European CLT Network — https://www.clteurope.org/tools-resources ; Terre de Liens (via ScienceDirect review) — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0264837725003710 ; Agrarian Trust — https://www.agrariantrust.org/ ; Farmland Access Legal Toolkit (Agrarian Commons guide) — https://farmlandaccess.org/guide-to-creating-an-agrarian-commons/

## 4. Farmland-protection regimes as settlement gates

These are the *hard* gates — they decide whether a dwelling/community can exist on ag land at all, independent of who owns it.

### BC Agricultural Land Reserve (ALR) [Kootenays]
- **What's current:** ALR caps the principal residence at **500 m²**. Since 2024, **additional residence** rules eased: on parcels ≤40 ha, a second residence of **≤90 m²** is permitted without an ALC non-adhering application (if primary ≤500 m² and local government allows); on parcels >40 ha, second home up to **186 m²**. Second dwelling may be a garden suite, a unit within an ag building, or a coach house over a one-storey garage.
- **2024–2025 changes:** **B.C. Reg. 244/2024**, amended by **B.C. Reg. 38/2025**. ALC Information Bulletin 05 revised 13 June 2024; additional-residence design requirements updated 19 June 2025 (guidelines Oct 2025).
- **Reciprocity read:** Loosened for *one* extra dwelling — but the ALR remains fundamentally hostile to *multi-household community* settlement on farmland. A 6-family cohousing group on ALR land still needs ALC approval and faces the multi-dwelling wall. This is the binding constraint for Kootenays community formation.
- **Sources:** ALR Use Regulation (BC Laws, official) — https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/civix/document/id/complete/statreg/30_2019 ; ALC "Housing Legislation in the ALR" PDF — https://www.alc.gov.bc.ca/app/uploads/sites/763/2024/07/Housing-Legislation-in-the-ALR.pdf ; Additional Residential Structures Guidelines (2025) — https://www.alc.gov.bc.ca/app/uploads/sites/763/2025/10/Additional-Residential-Structures-in-the-ALR-Guidelines.pdf

### Oregon Exclusive Farm Use (EFU) / ORS 215 [Cascadia]
- **What's current:** ORS 215 governs dwellings in EFU zones — farm dwellings require the parcel be managed as part of a farm operation and meet farm-income standards (ORS 215.279) and lot-size minimums (ORS 215.203/.213/.283). Non-farm and lot-of-record dwellings are tightly constrained. EFU is one of the strictest farm-dwelling regimes in the US.
- **2024–2025 changes:** DLCD ran a multi-phase rulemaking workplan (2023–2025): **2024 documentation standard** using IRS tax-return receipts as minimum income proof for farm-dwelling/farm-stand standards (does *not* change the income thresholds themselves); Phase 1 **Farm Stand rulemaking** adopted; future phases queued for agritourism, replacement/non-farm dwellings. Statutory tag **2025 c.355 §1** appears against ORS 215. **[PARTIAL — substance of 2025 c.355 unverified; confirm before citing as a dwelling-rule change.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** No material loosening for *new community* dwellings on EFU; the income-documentation change tightens proof. Cascadia community settlement remains gated hard by EFU — non-EFU or rural-residential zoning is the realistic path.
- **Sources:** ORS 215 index (Oregon Legislature) — https://www.oregonlegislature.gov/bills_laws/ors/ors215.html ; ORS 215.279 (farm income standard) — https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_215.279 ; ORS 215.283 — https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_215.283

### Québec CPTAQ Green Zone [Québec Eastern Townships]
- **What's current:** ~63% of Québec (incl. much of the Eastern Townships) is a protected agricultural "Green Zone" under CQLR c. P-41.1. Any non-agricultural use, subdivision, or new residence in the zone requires **CPTAQ authorization** — a high bar. **Bill 86 (2025, §1)** *tightens* this: non-farmer acquirers now need CPTAQ sign-off demonstrating agricultural intent, plus the fund/legal-person moratorium.
- **Reciprocity read:** Eastern Townships farmland settlement got **harder** in 2025 for non-farming communities; a working-farm collective is favored, a residential-intent group is squeezed.
- **Sources:** LégisQuébec P-41.1 — https://www.legisquebec.gouv.qc.ca/en/document/cs/p-41.1 ; Bill 86 (see §1).

### France — SAFER pre-emption + Sempastous [Cévennes]
- **What's current:** SAFER (public-mission private bodies) hold a **2-month pre-emption right** on farmland sales; can pre-empt to redirect land to farmers. The **Sempastous Law** (in force **1 April 2023**) added prefectural prior-approval for certain share transfers, to stop land-grabbing via corporate-share deals.
- **2025 development:** In the night of **11–12 March 2025** the National Assembly adopted a bill (Peio Dufau) **strengthening SAFER pre-emption** — notably allowing SAFER to **split a transfer between agricultural land and residential buildings**, closing the "buy the farmhouse-plus-land as one lot to dodge pre-emption" loophole. **[STATUS: adopted by the Assembly — verify final passage (Senate + promulgation) before citing as law.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** France is tightening *against* non-farmer diversion of farmland — good for genuine agricultural communities working via Terre de Liens, harder for a residential-intent buyer trying to bundle a farmhouse.
- **Sources:** Réussir (députés renforcent la préemption Safer) — https://www.reussir.fr/foncier-agricole-les-deputes-renforcent-les-droits-de-preemption-des-safer ; LCP Assemblée nationale — https://lcp.fr/actualites/agriculture-les-deputes-adoptent-une-proposition-de-loi-visant-a-lutter-contre-la

### Germany — Grundstückverkehrsgesetz (GrdstVG) [Saxony-Anhalt]
- **What's current:** GrdstVG (1961) requires authority approval for sales of agricultural/forest land; approval can be denied where the sale causes "unhealthy distribution of land" (i.e., a non-farmer outbidding a farmer). Agricultural pre-emption favors active farmers.
- **2024–2026 change:** **Saxony-Anhalt's** long-pending **Agrarstrukturgesetz** (land-market reform bill, submitted to the Landtag Nov 2020) aims to curb dominant regional land positions and regulate **share deals** in agribusinesses. **[STATUS: I could not confirm the Saxony-Anhalt Agrarstrukturgesetz was enacted 2024–2026 — treat as still-pending/unverified.]** Federal GrdstVG most recently amended 2026 (housekeeping). **[PARTIAL — verify the 2026 amendment substance.]**
- **Reciprocity read:** Saxony-Anhalt farmland strongly favors active farmers over outside capital; a non-farming community faces the "unhealthy distribution" denial risk. Working through an established farm or cooperative is the reciprocal path.
- **Sources:** GrdstVG (gesetze-im-internet, official) — https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/grdstvg/BJNR010910961.html ; IAMO on Saxony-Anhalt reform — https://www.iamo.de/en/press/press-releases/article/saxony-anhalts-land-market-reform-bill-is-it-viable ; Sachsen-Anhalt Bodenmarkt portal — https://mwl.sachsen-anhalt.de/landwirtschaft/bodenmarkt

### Spain — Galicia Ley 11/2021 land-recovery (context for Galicia/Asturias)
- **What's current:** **Ley 11/2021** (Galicia, 14 May 2021) fights farmland abandonment via a **Banco de Terras** (land bank), **Aldeas Modelo** (model villages — recovers land around rural nuclei), and **agroforestry polygons** (voluntary rent/exchange/sale mechanisms to put abandoned land into production). This is an *opening* regime — it actively channels abandoned land to willing users.
- **2024 change:** **Decreto 170/2024** (1 July 2024) created the law's Evaluation & Monitoring Commission; **July 2025** resolution opened aid for legal/technical/notarial costs of mobilization instruments (exchanges, private-initiative agroforestry polygons).
- **Reciprocity read:** Galicia is arguably the **most settlement-*friendly*** European target region for a community willing to farm — the state is *seeking* people to take up abandoned parcels via Aldeas Modelo. This is a reciprocity-aligned opening.
- **Sources:** Ley 11/2021 (BOE) — https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2021-10669 ; consolidated (Xunta) — https://xeg.xunta.gal/es/normativa/ley-112021-de-14-de-mayo-de-recuperacion-de-la-tierra-agraria-de-galicia-consolidado-29 ; AGADER recuperación de tierras — https://agader.xunta.gal/es/recuperacion-de-tierras

### Italy — South Tirol conventioned housing (context for South Tirol)
- **What's current:** In the autonomous province of Bolzano/South Tirol, **conventioned (subsidized) housing** may only be *occupied* by persons with ≥5 years' primary residence in South Tirol or employment in the province; cannot be used as holiday/second homes; occupancy required within 1 year. Restriction generally permanent (older stock may clear after 20 years).
- **Reciprocity read:** A strong *residence-belonging* gate on a large share of the housing stock. An incoming community must include the province in daily life (residence/work), not treat it as a retreat. Reciprocity is written into the deed restriction.
- **Sources:** SANLUI Real Estate (conventioned apartments) — https://www.sanlui.immo/en/appartamenti-convenzionati-in-alto-adige-cosa-devono-sapere-gli-acquirenti/

## 5. Indigenous land, treaty & co-stewardship developments (2024–2026)

Directly relevant to the reciprocity spine: these developments define *whose* land the North American regions actually are, and set the co-stewardship expectations any arriving community should meet.

- **Mi'kma'ki (Nova Scotia):** **Toqi'maliaptmu'k Arrangement** signed **10 December 2025** — Mi'kmaq of Nova Scotia + Parks Canada co-management/shared-stewardship of all Parks Canada places in NS (except Sable Island NPR), 10-year term, implementing Peace & Friendship Treaty rights. Follows the Marshall (1999) moderate-livelihood affirmation of the 1760–61 treaties. **Reciprocity signal:** shared governance is now the operating norm on public heritage lands; treaties are unceded and live.
  - Source (Canada.ca, official) — https://www.canada.ca/en/parks-canada/news/2025/12/the-mikmaq-of-nova-scotia-and-the-government-of-canada-sign-co-management-arrangement.html ; Kwilmu'kw Maw-klusuaqn — https://mikmaqrights.com/
- **Sinixt / Ktunaxa / Syilx (Kootenays, BC):** **Columbia River Treaty** modernization — Canada–US **agreement-in-principle July 2024**; **Ktunaxa, Secwépemc, Syilx Okanagan** are full members of the Canadian negotiating team and receive **5% each of downstream-power-benefit revenue** under interim agreements. **Critical gap:** the **Sinixt** (declared "extinct" in Canada in 1956; affirmed as an Aboriginal people with s.35 rights by the SCC in *R v Desautel*, 2021, but organized via the Colville Confederated Tribes in the US) are **excluded from the treaty table** despite direct territorial impact. **Reciprocity signal:** in the Kootenays, "whose land" is *actively contested between recognized and unrecognized nations* — an arriving community cannot assume a single host authority.
  - Sources: BC Gov (AIP July 2024) — https://news.gov.bc.ca/releases/2024EMLI0041-001094 ; Okanagan Nation Alliance — https://syilx.org/agreements-address-columbia-river-treaty-impacts-on-indigenous-nations/ ; Autonomous Sinixt — https://sinixt.org/
- **Abenaki / Ndakina (Vermont):** **Vermont Land Trust–Abenaki land-access affirmation** (VLT recognizes Abenaki as original stewards; Abenaki may use VLT lands for cultural/food/medicine gathering without specific permission). **Ongoing legitimacy dispute:** Quebec-based **Odanak and Wôlinak** First Nations released a **2025 genealogical report** contesting the ancestry/legitimacy of Vermont's four state-recognized tribes (Elnu, Koasek, Missisquoi, Nulhegan). **Reciprocity signal:** in Vermont, *which* Abenaki body is the legitimate host is itself contested — a settling community must navigate a live recognition dispute, not a settled land-acknowledgment.
  - Sources: Atowi (VLT–Abenaki agreement) — https://www.atowi.org/blog/vermont-land-trust-abenaki-land-access-agreement ; VTDigger (recognition dispute) — https://vtdigger.org/2023/11/14/a-false-narrative-abenaki-leaders-dispute-the-legitimacy-of-vermonts-state-recognized-tribes/
- **Cherokee / Qualla Boundary (Southern Appalachians, NC):** EBCI trust lands held by the US for the Eastern Band. **2021:** EBCI reacquired ~309 acres including **Kituwah** (mother town), gaining trust status. Ongoing incremental trust-land expansion. **[No major 2024–2026 land-back ruling verified — treat as continuation, not a new event.]**
  - Source: Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians (Wikipedia overview) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Band_of_Cherokee_Indians
- **Ho-Chunk (Driftless, WI):** **Dane County** purchased **165 acres** (~$6.41M, Conservation Fund), placed a permanent conservation easement, and **donated the land to the Ho-Chunk Nation** (2024–2025) — protecting rare burial-mound groupings along the Yahara River corridor. A concrete county-level land-back transaction in the Driftless region.
  - Sources: PBS Wisconsin — https://pbswisconsin.org/news-item/dane-county-returns-165-acres-to-the-ho-chunk-nation/ ; Cap Times — https://captimes.com/news/government/165-acre-6-4m-deal-aims-to-protect-burial-mounds-and-wildlife-in-dane-county/article_22d7940d-e7bb-46e2-a59d-ca203d0fdf64.html
- **Osage (Ozarks, AR):** Osage historically ceded Missouri/Arkansas lands (1808). Recent Osage activity is **Missouri casino development** (Lake Ozark, land-into-trust application, Treasury review Oct–Nov 2024), **not** an Arkansas/Ozarks land-back event. **[No Arkansas-Ozarks Osage land development verified 2024–2026.]**
  - Source: NPS Osage — https://www.nps.gov/articles/osage.htm
- **Pueblo / acequia (Northern New Mexico):** Wave of **tribal water-rights settlements** advancing through Congress 2024–2025 (e.g., **Ohkay Owingeh Rio Chama Water Rights Settlement Act** — trust fund for US/NM/Española/acequia associations/Ohkay Owingeh). Acequias set **2025 legislative priorities** and backed a **$40M** State Engineer request tied to settlement costs finalizing the oldest (Pueblo + acequia) water rights. **Reciprocity signal:** in Northern New Mexico, *water* is the operative land-tenure question — Pueblo senior rights and acequia community-ditch rights are being legally finalized; an arriving community's water access is downstream (literally) of these settlements.
  - Sources: Source New Mexico (acequia 2025 priorities) — https://sourcenm.com/2024/11/29/new-mexicos-acequias-outline-2025-legislative-priorities/ ; Rep. Vasquez (settlements) — https://vasquez.house.gov/media/in-the-news/12-tribes-and-pueblos-new-mexico-could-benefit-pending-water-rights-settlements
- **Zapotec / Mixtec / Chatino comunal (Oaxaca):** **2024 constitutional reform to Article 2** recognized Indigenous communities as rights-bearing subjects — **but failed to fold ejidos/comunidades agrarias into the Indigenous institutional framework**, leaving a gap: no national-level legal protection of Indigenous *territories* as such. Simultaneously the **Salomón Jara state government's 2024 revenue law** offered fiscal incentives pushing comuneros/ejidatarios toward **privatizing/titling** communal land — read by autonomy advocates as pressure against communal tenure. **Foreigners cannot hold ejido/comunal land**; entry is only by assembly consent. **Reciprocity signal:** Oaxaca is simultaneously *strengthening* symbolic Indigenous rights and *fiscally eroding* communal tenure — the deepest and most contested reciprocity terrain of the 20 regions.
  - Sources: Oñati Socio-Legal Series (land/law/indigeneity in Mexico) — https://opo.iisj.net/index.php/osls/article/view/2226 ; It's Going Down (state/capital vs communal autonomy) — https://itsgoingdown.org/unsolvable-tension-state-and-capital-against-communal-autonomy-in-oaxaca/

## 6. Net verdict per region — is the door opening or closing?

Legend: **↑ = materially easier** to lawfully settle 2024–2026; **↓ = materially harder**; **→ = broadly unchanged** (existing hard gate stable).

### Europe
| Region | Direction | Specific legal reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Galicia (ES)** | **↑** | Ley 11/2021 + Decreto 170/2024 + 2025 aid — state actively channels abandoned land to willing users (Aldeas Modelo, Banco de Terras, agroforestry polygons). Most settlement-*seeking* regime. |
| **Asturias (ES)** | **→** | No blanket non-EU ban; abandonment/land-mobilization dynamics similar to Galicia but without Galicia's specific recovery law. Open in principle. |
| **Alentejo (PT)** | **↓ (for non-EU)** | Real-estate Golden Visa route abolished (2023) — property purchase no longer grants residency; removes the non-EU pathway that drove rural buying. Purchase itself still open to EU buyers. |
| **Cévennes (FR)** | **↓ (for non-farmers)** | Sempastous (2023) + March 2025 SAFER-strengthening (splits land/dwelling lots) tighten against non-farmer diversion. Terre de Liens is the reciprocal opening. |
| **Transylvania (RO)** | **→ / ↓** | Law 175/2020 already closed direct non-resident farmland purchase (5-yr domicile + 5-yr ag activity + 75% income); Law 116/2024 churned but did not reopen. SRL route narrowed. |
| **Saxony-Anhalt (DE)** | **→** | GrdstVG "unhealthy distribution" gate stable; Agrarstrukturgesetz reform still pending/unverified. Favors active farmers. |
| **South Tirol (IT)** | **→** | Conventioned-housing 5-yr-residence occupancy gate stable — strong belonging requirement, unchanged. |
| **rural Estonia (EE)** | **→ (open for OECD)** | RAIA OECD carve-out keeps US/EU/EEA buyers unrestricted; only third-country (non-OECD) buyers face the residence test. Jan-2026 Russia/Belarus draft not yet law. |
| **Pembrokeshire (Wales/UK)** | **→ (favorable)** | One Planet Development remains live and is the single most community-settlement-friendly rural route; no verified 2024–2026 change. |
| **West Cork/Connemara (IE)** | **↓ (for non-locals)** | Draft Rural & Gaeltacht NPS (Jun 2026) sharpens local-need + Irish-language gates; easier for rooted/Irish-speaking households, harder for incoming non-locals. Still draft. |

### North America
| Region | Direction | Specific legal reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Cascadia (OR)** | **→ (hard gate)** | EFU/ORS 215 farm-dwelling regime unchanged in substance; 2024 income-documentation rule tightens proof. Foreign-ownership bill proposed, unverified. |
| **Vermont** | **↑ then contested** | Act 181 (2024) raised rural review threshold 6–10 → 50 units; but 2026 partial repeal (road rule + Tier 3 scrapped) shows volatility. Net loosening for now, unsettled. |
| **Southern Appalachians (NC)** | **→** | HB 463 foreign-adversary bill targets foreign governments, not domestic communities; unverified enactment. Qualla Boundary trust land expanding incrementally. |
| **Driftless (WI)** | **↓ (for foreign) / → (domestic)** | AB 218 (if enacted) cuts foreign cap 640 → 50 ac — but not law yet; domestic community formation unaffected. Ho-Chunk land-back active. |
| **Ozarks (AR)** | **↓ (for foreign) / → (domestic)** | Act 636/2023 (+ 2025 amendments, unverified) bars "prohibited foreign parties"; first state to enforce/divest. US-resident collectives unaffected. |
| **Northern New Mexico** | **→ (water-gated)** | Land purchase open, but Pueblo + acequia water-rights settlements (2024–2025) are the binding constraint on any new community's water access. |
| **Nova Scotia (CA)** | **↓** | Non-resident deed-transfer tax 5% → 10% (1 Apr 2025, residential incl. vacant land); PNP immigration allocation halved in 2025. Both close the door on non-resident arrival. 6-month-relocation exemption is the reciprocal escape hatch. |
| **Kootenays (BC, CA)** | **→ (hard gate) + contested title** | ALR eased *one* extra dwelling (Reg. 244/2024 → 38/2025) but multi-household community settlement still walled; Columbia River Treaty + Sinixt exclusion make "whose land" actively contested. |
| **Québec Eastern Townships (CA)** | **↓** | Bill 86 (assent Mar 2025) + retroactive Dec-2024 fund moratorium tighten CPTAQ Green Zone acquisition against non-farmers/legal persons. Working farms favored. |
| **Oaxaca (MX)** | **→ / contested** | Comunal/ejido tenure closed to foreigners (assembly-consent only); 2024 Art. 2 reform strengthens symbolic rights while state fiscal policy pressures privatization. Deepest reciprocity gate. |

**Pattern:** The clearest *openings* (Galicia, Pembrokeshire OPD, Vermont's higher thresholds, Nova Scotia's relocation exemption) all reward **actual residence + agricultural/ecological commitment + collective or low-impact form.** The clearest *closings* (Nova Scotia non-resident tax, Québec Bill 86, Romania, French SAFER, US foreign-ownership laws) all target **non-resident, speculative, or capital-only acquisition.** For a community whose thesis is *belonging over 50–100 years*, the legislative wind of 2024–2026 is at its back — provided it arrives as residents-who-farm-in-common, not buyers-who-hold.
