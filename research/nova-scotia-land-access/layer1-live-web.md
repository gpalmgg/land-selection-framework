## Firecrawl (deep crawl — primary source)

**Source:** [Nova Scotia Non-Resident Provincial Deed Transfer Tax Guidelines (PDF)](https://novascotia.ca/finance/en/home/taxation/tax101/docs/Nova-Scotia-Provincial-Non-resident-Deed-Transfer-Tax-Guidelines.pdf), Nova Scotia Department of Finance and Treasury Board, Provincial Tax Policy and Administration Division, **released August 2025** — scraped directly, HTTP 200.

Key facts confirmed straight from the primary document:

- **Rate/date:** For Agreements of Purchase and Sale signed on/after **1 April 2025** (and other transfers without an APS on/after that date), the tax amount is per the "Tax Amount" section referenced at 10%.
- **Scope definition:** "According to the Assessment Act, a residential property is one that is used, or intended to be used, for residential purposes... a taxable residential property is one that has 3 or fewer dwelling units. **This includes vacant land considered to be residential property.**"
- **Commercial/Resource carve-out mechanism:** "**Commercial and Resource Properties**: If a property being transferred is not currently classified as residential but it is intended to be used for residential purposes in the future, the PDTT is payable (providing no other exemption is applicable)." — This is the load-bearing nuance: agricultural/resource-classified land is **not automatically exempt by virtue of being agricultural** — it is out of scope only because it isn't classified as residential *and* is not intended for future residential use. If a non-resident buyer intends to convert agricultural/resource land to residential use, the tax can still apply.
- **The enumerated "Tax Exemptions" list (16 items)** contains NO explicit "agricultural land" exemption clause. The 16 exemptions are: pre-April-2022 agreements, >3-dwelling-unit properties, new-resident relocation (within 6 months), spousal/common-law transfers, divorce-related transfers, spousal trusts, executor-to-beneficiary transfers, executor-to-spousal-trust, intestate succession, post-death family transfers, alter-ego trusts, no-change-in-beneficial-ownership trust transfers, foreclosure-related transfers, deed corrections, registered-charity transfers, and Crown transfers.
- **Correction to the WebSearch/Brave/Perplexity "agricultural exemption" framing:** none of the three other engines' snippets, nor the Perplexity synthesis, precisely reflect the primary document's mechanism. The correct statement is: **agricultural/non-residential land is out of scope of the tax's definition of "taxable residential property," not covered by a named exemption** — a materially different legal mechanism (definitional exclusion vs. explicit exemption) with the same practical effect for a buyer with no residential-conversion intent, but a different effect for a buyer intending future residential development on agricultural/resource land (still taxable).
