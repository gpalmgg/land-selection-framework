# Regenerative Ecosystem — West Cork / Connemara, Ireland

**Headline finding:** Ireland has a small but disproportionately influential ecovillage and regen network anchored by Cloughjordan (Tipperary, Europe's largest ecovillage at 67 acres) and the Hollies (West Cork). West Cork in particular has been a long-running magnet for regenerative-agriculture practitioners, organic horticulture, and small-batch food enterprises. Connemara's regen scene is thinner but has Atlantic-rim agroforestry and machair-restoration projects of growing importance.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Global Ecovillage Network entries in Ireland: **~10 active community/regen entries** including Cloughjordan, The Hollies (Enniskeane, West Cork), An Choill (Roscommon), and Birchcraft (Wexford) — ecovillage.org Ireland directory 2025. Cloughjordan Ecovillage hosts ~150 residents across 132 designed dwellings (Cloughjordan Ecovillage website 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- Cloughjordan Ecovillage (Tipperary, founded 1999, residents from 2009) — Europe's largest planned ecovillage, runs Ireland's first Community Supported Agriculture farm and an extensive permaculture/woodland system.
- The Hollies Centre for Practical Sustainability (Enniskeane, West Cork) — long-running permaculture education farm and ecobuild centre.
- Organic Centre, Rossinver (Leitrim) — adjacent though not in this region; relevant as the national hub for organic training; mention as reference network.
- West Cork foodscape: Neal's Yard Dairy origin connections, sheep's-milk and goat's-milk artisan dairying around Skibbereen and Castletownshend.
- Connemara-area regen activity: Brigit's Garden (Roscahill, Co. Galway) — biodiversity garden and regen-education site; Atlantic Salmon Trust and Salmon Watch Ireland restoration work on Owenmore/Erriff catchments.
- Active 2024–2026 funder/programme list (verified): **CAP Strategic Plan Ireland 2023–2027** (Department of Agriculture, Food & the Marine — €9.8bn envelope; eco-schemes, ACRES agri-environment scheme), **LEADER** local-action groups (West Cork Development Partnership, Forum Connemara), **Just Transition Fund** (Bord na Móna midlands regions), **EPA Green Enterprise Programme**, **Pobal** (community-development funder), **Hometree** (West-of-Ireland native woodland charity, philanthropic regen partner).
- ACRES (Agri-Climate Rural Environment Scheme) launched 2023 — Ireland's flagship agri-environment payment with 55,000 farmer participants by mid-2024; co-operative-area projects relevant to community land projects.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Ireland's regen funding is real but routed through national channels with rigid eligibility — most CAP/ACRES payments require an Irish-registered farmer and a herd number. Foreign-led community projects typically partner with an existing farmer for grant access. The cultural integration story in West Cork is well-trodden (multi-generational inflow since the 1970s "blow-ins") and easier than in many regions. Connemara is harder — Irish-language commitment and Gaeltacht social fabric require deeper integration than newcomers often anticipate.
