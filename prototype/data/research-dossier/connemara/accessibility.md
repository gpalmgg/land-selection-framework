# Accessibility + Demographics — West Cork / Connemara, Ireland

**Headline finding:** Both West Cork and Connemara combine genuine rurality with surprisingly good baseline connectivity — Ireland's small geography puts Cork or Galway cities within 1–2 hours of even peripheral villages, and the country has invested heavily in National Broadband Plan rural fibre rollout. Demographics are positive on the West Cork side (in-migration from Cork City and Dublin); Connemara's Gaeltacht core has seen modest decline offset by peripheral growth.

**Key data point (with vintage):** West Cork population density 2022 Census: **~29 inhabitants/km²** (Cork County rural West LEA aggregate, CSO Census 2022). Connemara/Galway West density 2022: **~22 inhabitants/km²** (CSO Census 2022). Ireland national density: ~73/km². Median age in rural West Cork 2022: ~42 years; Connemara Gaeltacht core: ~43 years (CSO Census 2022 small-area breakdown).

**Supporting facts:**
- Tertiary hospitals: Cork University Hospital (Wilton, Cork city — within 1–1.5 h of most of West Cork); Galway University Hospital (within 1–1.5 h of most of Connemara); Bantry General Hospital (West Cork regional); Letterkenny / Sligo for north-west fringes.
- Airports: Cork (ORK) and Shannon (SNN) within 1–2 h of West Cork; Knock (NOC) and Shannon for Connemara; Dublin (DUB) is 3.5–4 h drive from both. Aer Lingus + Ryanair routes connect to major European hubs.
- Road density: N71 along West Cork coast, N59 looping Connemara — both single-carriageway national roads. Motorways do not extend into either; expect 60–80 km/h average speeds.
- Internet coverage: National Broadband Plan (NBP) rolled out high-speed fibre to >300,000 rural premises by end-2024 — coverage is now real in both regions, though some headlands and islands remain on fixed-wireless. ComReg quarterly broadband update Q4 2024.
- Electrification: 100% grid; ESB Networks resilient but storm-prone on the Atlantic coast. Microgeneration regulated under SEAI clean-export-guarantee scheme.
- Demographic trend: West Cork experienced significant in-migration through 2020–2024 (remote-work + lifestyle); Connemara Gaeltacht core declining slowly but supported by Údarás na Gaeltachta investment in language-economy enterprises.
- Schools: rural primary network reasonably intact in both regions (West Cork has a number of healthy small NS schools); Gaeltacht schools in Connemara are Irish-medium (Gaelscoileanna) which is a positive for Irish-speaking families and a barrier for non-Irish-speaking newcomers.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The two areas differ sharply in cultural texture despite similar density. West Cork has been receiving "blow-ins" for 50+ years and accommodates non-Irish newcomers with minimal friction; Connemara's Gaeltacht core operates in Irish socially and educationally — outsider families need to commit to Irish learning to integrate. Storm exposure on the Atlantic coast is real: confirm grid resilience and back-up power for any community project siting. Rural fibre rollout has materially changed remote-work viability — the 2018-vintage "rural Ireland is offline" narrative is no longer accurate.
