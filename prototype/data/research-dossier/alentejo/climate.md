# Climate — Alentejo, Portugal

**Headline finding:** Alentejo is projected to be one of the hotter, drier corners of Europe by mid-century, with mean annual temperatures climbing ~2°C above 1995–2014 baselines under moderate-emissions pathways. Practitioners siting projects here are betting on adaptation strategies (shade infrastructure, drought-tolerant agroecology, deep-cool earthworks), not on climate stability.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Mean annual temperature projection 2041–2060 under SSP2-4.5: **~19.5°C** (multi-model ensemble mean from WorldClim CMIP6 v2.1, downscaled bioclimatic data, 2024 release; consistent with the 2.7–2.9°C warming envelope reported in IPMA's regional climate scenarios, 2023).

**Supporting facts:**
- Maximum temperature of the warmest month projected at 34.5–36°C by 2050 under SSP2-4.5 (BIO5, WorldClim CMIP6 v2.1, 2024).
- Heat-related mortality studies for Alentejo project a sharp rise in summer extreme-heat days through 2050 under intermediate scenarios (Gomes et al., 2024, PMC11662523).
- High-resolution CMIP6 ensemble for Portugal indicates summer warming of +2.0–2.5°C and a ~10–15% reduction in wet-day frequency for southern Portugal by 2041–2060 under SSP2-4.5 (Nature Scientific Reports, 2024).

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
For a settlement designed to flourish across 2050–2100, Alentejo's climate trajectory matters more than its current state. The current state is already hot-summer Mediterranean (Csa); the trajectory is toward longer, drier summers and more frequent extreme-heat events. Building strategies that succeed here at 2025 may struggle by 2055 — shade infrastructure, courtyard and earth-tempered architecture, drought-tolerant species selection, and aggressive water-conservation systems become not optional but foundational. The flip side: winter conditions remain mild and frost-free across most of the region, extending the working-outdoors season and reducing heating demand.
