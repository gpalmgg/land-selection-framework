# Political Stability — Alentejo, Portugal

**Headline finding:** Portugal sits in the top tier of EU democratic stability with no recent conflict events in or near the Alentejo region. The 2024 minority-government dynamic (Aliança Democrática coalition) introduces some legislative friction but no institutional risk; civil-society response capacity is strong nationally and locally well-organised through municipal associations.

**Key data point (with vintage):** V-Dem Electoral Democracy Index for Portugal 2023: **0.86** (top quintile globally; published V-Dem v14, March 2024). UCDP recorded conflict events within Alentejo 2019–2024: **zero** (UCDP GED dataset, v24.1). INFORM Risk 2024 for Portugal: **2.1/10** (low-risk band; INFORM 2024 release).

**Supporting facts:**
- Portugal is a consolidated EU and NATO member with continuous democratic governance since 1974 (Carnation Revolution).
- March 2024 legislative elections produced minority AD government under PM Luís Montenegro; mid-2024 saw orderly budget negotiation, no extra-constitutional disruption.
- Chega party rose to ~18% of the vote in 2024, third-largest force; institutional impact has so far been parliamentary, not street-level.
- Civil-society infrastructure in Alentejo includes ADRAL (Agência de Desenvolvimento Regional do Alentejo) and ANIMAR (national network of local development associations) — both active conveners for community-level coordination.
- Government continuity for the 4-year electoral cycle expected; next scheduled legislative elections 2028.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
For long-horizon community projects, Portugal's combination of stable institutions, low-conflict environment, and locally responsive municipalities is unusually favourable. The political risk is not regime instability — it is policy churn around foreign investment (the Golden Visa programme was restructured in October 2023, removing real-estate eligibility). Project-relevant policy can shift between governments; secure freehold title remains constitutionally protected regardless.
