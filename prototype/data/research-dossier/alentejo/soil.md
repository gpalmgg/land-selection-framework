# Soil & Biodiversity — Alentejo, Portugal

**Headline finding:** Alentejo's dominant landscape is the *montado* — a cork-oak / holm-oak silvopastoral system carrying high biodiversity value and EU Habitats Directive protection, but resting on thin, low-fertility Mediterranean soils with modest organic-carbon stocks. Regenerative gains here come from rebuilding SOC and tree-cover, not from inheriting rich ground.

**Key data point (with vintage):** SoilGrids 2.0 (ISRIC, 2020 release) reports topsoil (0–5 cm) soil organic carbon for most of inland Alentejo in the range **~15–25 g/kg**, with sub-30-cm SOC stocks typically 30–50 t/ha — characteristic of degraded Mediterranean rangelands, well below temperate-Atlantic baselines (60–120 t/ha).

**Supporting facts:**
- Dominant soil types: Cambisols, Luvisols, and shallow Leptosols on schist and granite parent material; depth is frequently <50 cm in upland zones (European Soil Data Centre, 2020).
- *Montado* (cork-oak wood-pasture) is listed as EU Habitats Directive habitat 6310 and underpins multiple Natura 2000 Special Areas of Conservation across Alentejo (Bugalho et al., 2011; Plieninger et al., 2018).
- Portugal supplies ~50% of global cork; Alentejo holds the bulk of the national cork oak area, with traditional management cycles of 9-year bark harvest (APCOR / ICNF data, 2023).
- Land-use history: extensive cereal cultivation through the 20th century degraded SOC on cleared lands; abandonment and *montado* restoration since the 1990s has produced measurable but slow SOC recovery (Costa et al., PLOS One, 2019).

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
A practitioner inheriting Alentejo land is usually inheriting degraded ground under a (relatively) intact tree canopy, not the other way around. The cork-oak overstory is the durable asset; the soil beneath it is the rebuild project. Holistic grazing, keyline water spreading, and tree-crop integration can plausibly add 1–3 g/kg SOC per decade, but Mediterranean dryness throttles biology and gains compound slowly. Buying land *with* mature montado is materially different from buying cleared ground next door — the carbon, biodiversity, and microclimate dividends are decades-deep and irreplaceable on a settlement timeline.
