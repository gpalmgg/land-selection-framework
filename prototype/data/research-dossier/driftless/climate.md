# Climate — Driftless Area (Southwest Wisconsin), United States

**Headline finding:** The Driftless Area sits in a cold continental climate (Dfa/Dfb borderline) with a current mean annual temperature of approximately 9.3°C at La Crosse — cool summers, cold winters, reliable four-season rhythm. CMIP6 projections under SSP2-4.5 point to roughly +2.3°C warming by 2041–2060 relative to the 1991–2020 baseline, lifting the projected mid-century mean annual temperature to approximately 11.5–12°C. The trajectory is toward longer growing seasons, milder winters, and meaningfully more intense summer heat events — a substantial but not catastrophic shift compared with southern European regions facing much steeper warming.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Projected mean annual temperature 2041–2060 under SSP2-4.5: **~11.5–12°C** (derived from: current baseline La Crosse 1991–2020 normal = 9.3°C / 48.8°F per Wisconsin State Climatology Office; + projected statewide annual warming of +4.1°F / +2.3°C under SSP2-4.5 for 2041–2060 relative to 1991–2020, per Wisconsin Initiative on Climate Change Impacts WICCI downscaled CMIP6 ensemble, 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- Current 1991–2020 climate normal for La Crosse (nearest first-order station to SW Wisconsin Driftless): mean annual temperature 48.8°F (9.3°C), mean annual high 58.6°F (14.8°C), mean annual low 39.1°F (3.9°C); annual precipitation 35.23 inches (895 mm); annual snowfall 46.3 inches (Wisconsin State Climatology Office, 2024).
- WICCI CMIP6 downscaled ensemble projects +4.1°F (+2.3°C) annual average warming for Wisconsin under SSP2-4.5 for 2041–2060 vs. 1991–2020 baseline, with winter nights warming the most (+5.5°F / +3.1°C) and summer days warming +4.4°F (+2.4°C).
- Number of 90°F+ (32°C+) days per year in Wisconsin projected to rise from 9 (current) to 26 by mid-century; sub-zero nights expected to decrease, extending shoulder-season growing windows (WICCI, 2024).
- The Fifth National Climate Assessment (NCA5, 2023) reports Great Lakes region mid-century air temperature increase of 3–6°F (1.7–3.3°C) under intermediate emissions scenarios, consistent with WICCI projections.
- Driftless Area growing season (last-frost to first-frost) currently approximately 150–165 days; projected to lengthen by 2–3 weeks by 2050, opening expanded perennial-crop and multi-harvest windows.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The Driftless climate story is meaningfully different from European candidates in two respects. First, the baseline is cold: even at projected mid-century temperatures, winter infrastructure requirements (insulation, passive solar, root cellars, livestock winter feed) remain genuine design constraints — unlike Alentejo or Cevennes, a practitioner here cannot neglect the cold half of the year. Second, the trajectory is arguably the most benign in this framework: a warming of ~2.3°C is substantial but leaves the region in a recognisably temperate band, and warming winters reduce heating demand and extend growing seasons. The growing risk is not heat stress but precipitation volatility — dryer summers are not heavily signaled for Wisconsin as they are for southern Europe, but more intense rain events interspersed with dry spells are projected, demanding good soil infiltration design. The Driftless's karst and deep-coulee topography handles this better than flat conventionally-tilled land.
