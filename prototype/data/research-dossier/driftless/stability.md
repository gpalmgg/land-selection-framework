# Stability — Driftless Area (Southwest Wisconsin), United States

**Headline finding:** The Driftless Area records zero UCDP GED events within the relevant search radius. The UCDP Georeferenced Event Dataset (v24.1) tracks organised armed violence resulting in at least one direct death — the entire Upper Midwest (Wisconsin, Minnesota, Iowa, Illinois) has no recorded events in the UCDP database, which covers state-based conflict, non-state conflict, and one-sided violence at the organised-actor level. The broader US political environment has elevated social polarisation indicators since 2016, and rural Wisconsin has experienced some of this — but it does not manifest as organised armed conflict at any UCDP-relevant level. The stability picture is excellent by any cross-national benchmark.

**Key data point (with vintage):** UCDP GED v24.1 events within ~200 km of coordinates [-90.8, 43.5] (Driftless Area centre): **0 events** (UCDP Georeferenced Event Dataset v24.1, Uppsala University, covering 1989–2023; United States Midwest has no qualifying organised violent events in the dataset; confirmed via UCDP country page for USA and Humanitarian Data Exchange dataset for US, accessed 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- The UCDP GED codebook defines an event as "an incident where armed force was used by an organised actor against another organized actor, or against civilians, resulting in at least 1 direct death at a specific location and a specific date." This threshold excludes interpersonal crime, riots, and protest — it captures only organised armed violence.
- The United States as a whole records a small number of UCDP events in other decades (e.g. some events related to domestic extremism), but none are located in the Upper Midwest agricultural region; the Driftless Area counties (Vernon, Crawford, Richland, Grant) have no entries.
- Wisconsin is consistently ranked among the safest US states for violent crime (FBI UCR data); rural Driftless counties have among the lowest crime rates in the state (Wisconsin DOJ uniform crime reports).
- Political climate note: rural Wisconsin is politically competitive and has experienced polarisation similar to broader US trends, but this does not translate to civil security risk at the UCDP threshold. Community members from urban backgrounds may notice a more conservative political context in some rural areas; Viroqua and La Farge (organic farming hub areas) are notable counter-exceptions with progressive/alternative communities.
- Natural hazard stability: the Driftless has periodic flood risk in coulee valleys (flash flooding from intense rainfall is the primary acute hazard), occasional severe winter storms, and very rare tornado events. These are manageable and well-documented; they are not existential risks.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The stability score here is the cleanest in the framework — no armed conflict, stable federal and state legal institutions, no reasonable 50-year scenario involving organised violence at the UCDP level. The practitioner lens should focus instead on lower-frequency but still real community-level stability factors: Wisconsin's political polarisation could create social friction for communities practicing alternative land use (notably, some rural neighbours have historically been suspicious of intentional communities or "back-to-the-land" movements); zoning boards and county planning commissions are the actual local governance layer a community encounters. The Driftless has a track record of being relatively tolerant of this — Dreamtime Village, New Forest Farm, and the Organic Valley co-op have all operated here for decades without significant community-conflict incidents.
