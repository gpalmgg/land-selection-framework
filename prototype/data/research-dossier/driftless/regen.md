# Regenerative Network — Driftless Area (Southwest Wisconsin), United States

**Headline finding:** The Driftless Area is one of the densest concentrations of organic, CSA, and regenerative farming in the United States, anchored by Organic Valley (world's largest organic farmer cooperative, headquartered in La Farge, WI), Marbleseed (formerly MOSES, the Midwest's primary organic farming support organization), New Forest Farm (a landmark 106-acre perennial agroforestry system in Viola), and the Driftless Region Food & Farm Project (University of Wisconsin Extension). Within 100 km of the Driftless core, more than 20 confirmed intentional community, permaculture, and regen-agriculture sites can be identified across Wisconsin and southern Minnesota, including Dreamtime Village (West Salem, WI), several Driftless-based cooperatives, and the broader La Farge / Viroqua / Cashton organic-farming cluster that supplies hundreds of CSA shares across the region.

**Supporting facts:**
- Organic Valley (CROPP Cooperative), founded 1988 in La Farge, WI, operates as the world's largest independent cooperative of organic family farmers with nearly 2,000 farmer-owners. Its founding and largest membership cluster is in the Kickapoo River Basin (Crawford, Vernon, Richland, Grant counties) — the geographic core of the Driftless. Headquarters campus in Cashton, WI.
- Wisconsin ranked second in the nation for total number of certified organic farms in 2021, with 1,455 certified operations statewide (USDA NASS, 2022). The Driftless four-county core accounts for a disproportionate share of this given the region's CSA and organic farming culture.
- Marbleseed (formerly Midwest Organic and Sustainable Education Service, MOSES), headquartered in Spring Valley, WI, is the region's primary organic farming education and support nonprofit; its annual Organic Farming Conference (La Crosse area) is one of the largest in the US.
- New Forest Farm, Viola, WI: 106-acre commercial-scale perennial agroforestry operation founded 1994 by Mark Shepard; one of the most-visited restoration agriculture demonstration sites in the US, with chestnuts, hazelnuts, apples, and 250,000+ trees planted.
- Dreamtime Village, West Salem, WI: long-established intentional community in the Driftless bioregion, focused on permaculture, art, media, and sustainable living.
- FairShare CSA Coalition (Madison-based) connects 80+ Driftless and Southwest Wisconsin CSA farms to urban consumers; Harmony Valley Farm (Viroqua) supplies 1,500+ CSA families weekly from May through December.
- The ic.org directory lists multiple intentional communities within 100 km of the Driftless core, concentrated in southwest Wisconsin and the Twin Cities / southeast Minnesota area.
- Driftless Region Food & Farm Project (University of Wisconsin Extension, driftless.wisc.edu) provides research and outreach specifically for this region's regenerative food system.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The regen network density in the Driftless is genuinely exceptional by US standards, and probably unmatched among rural North American regions of comparable size. A new intentional community here would not be arriving into a vacuum — it would be landing in a mature, overlapping network of organic farms, CSA operations, cooperative infrastructure, and practitioner knowledge that has been accumulating since the back-to-the-land wave of the 1970s. This has practical implications: local knowledge of what crops actually work (hazelnuts, elderberries, chestnuts, pastured dairy, market gardens), established wholesale and CSA channels, a regional supply chain for farm inputs, and social credibility with neighbours who have seen alternative farming work for decades. The flip side: land values in the Viroqua-La Farge-Cashton triangle have been rising partly because of this reputation, so the organic premium cuts both ways — it validates the vision but compresses affordability.
