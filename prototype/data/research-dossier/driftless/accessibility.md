# Accessibility — Driftless Area (Southwest Wisconsin), United States

**Headline finding:** The Driftless Area is deeply rural by US standards and exceptionally rural by the JRC GHSL population-density grid. The core Driftless counties — Vernon (county seat: Viroqua), Crawford (Prairie du Chien), and Richland (Richland Center) — have population densities of 15–38 persons/km², placing them in the JRC GHSL "rural" classification. This is a genuine low-density landscape. However, regional city access is meaningful: La Crosse, WI (population ~52,000) lies at the western Driftless edge; Madison, WI (~260,000) is approximately 90 minutes east; Minneapolis-St. Paul (~3.6 million) is 2.5–3 hours northwest. For a settlement seeking isolation with viable access to urban services, the Driftless threads this needle better than more remote regions.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Population density of Driftless core counties (2020 US Census): Vernon County **15.0 persons/km²** (38.8 persons/sq mile), Crawford County **10.9 persons/km²** (28.2 persons/sq mile) — consistent with JRC GHSL 2020 rural classification for this area (US Census Bureau, 2020 Decennial Census; JRC GHSL GHS-POP P2023A dataset, 2020 epoch).

**Supporting facts:**
- Vernon County (centred on Viroqua, the informal capital of the organic Driftless): population 30,714 as of 2020 Census; land area 792 sq miles; density 38.8 persons/sq mile (15.0/km²).
- Crawford County (Prairie du Chien on the Mississippi): population 16,113 as of 2020 Census; land area 571 sq miles; density 28.2 persons/sq mile (10.9/km²). Among the least densely populated counties in Wisconsin.
- Richland County: population 17,197 (2020 Census); land area ~587 sq miles; density ~29.3 persons/sq mile (~11.3/km²).
- The broader 5-county Driftless Wisconsin region (Crawford, Grant, Green, Lafayette, Richland) contains approximately 210,000 people across 4,288 square miles — a regional average of ~49 persons/sq mile (~18.9/km²).
- La Crosse, WI (population 52,680, 2020 Census) is the nearest city with hospital, airport (La Crosse Regional Airport, commercial service to Chicago, Minneapolis), significant retail, and university (UW–La Crosse); driving time from the Driftless core is 45–75 minutes.
- Madison, WI (population 258,054, 2020 Census; metro ~660,000) is the state capital with a major research university (UW–Madison), international airport (Dane County Regional), and full urban services; 1.5–2 hours from the Driftless core.
- Minneapolis-St. Paul MSA (~3.6 million, 2020 Census) is accessible via I-90 / I-94 in approximately 2.5–3 hours from Viroqua — realistic for periodic supply runs, medical specialists, and cultural access.
- No rail passenger service currently connects the Driftless core to regional cities; Amtrak Empire Builder passes through La Crosse (Chicago-Seattle route).

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Accessibility in the Driftless depends almost entirely on car ownership — there is no meaningful public transit in rural Driftless counties, and this is a genuine long-horizon vulnerability for communities that want to include members who don't or can't drive. The regional accessibility is actually quite good by rural US standards (La Crosse is closer than most regional cities are to comparable European rural regions), but intra-regional mobility is car-dependent. A community planning for 50+ years should think seriously about: (1) owning shared-pool vehicles rather than individual cars; (2) locating close enough to Viroqua, La Farge, or Westby to enable cycling for routine supply runs; (3) lobbying for or facilitating demand-responsive transit as electric vehicles and shared mobility evolve. The silver lining: the Driftless's isolation from urban sprawl pressure is what keeps it affordable and cohesive.
