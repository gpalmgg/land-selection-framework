# Energy — Québec Eastern Townships (Estrie), Canada

**Headline finding:** The Eastern Townships operate on one of the cleanest and cheapest electrical grids in North America. Hydro-Québec's supply is approximately 99% renewable (hydroelectric dominant, with growing wind capacity), and residential rates in Montréal ranked first or second cheapest among 22 major North American cities in 2024. The solar PV yield for Sherbrooke is approximately 1,088 kWh/kWp/year — meaningful but modest by global standards, comparable to central Germany or the UK midlands. The strategic energy picture for a regen settlement in Estrie is not solar-first; it is grid-connected hydropower-backed clean electricity at low cost, with solar as a resilience layer.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Solar PV yield for Sherbrooke, Estrie: **1,088 kWh/kWp/year** (Global Solar Atlas v2.7 / CanmetENERGY Photovoltaic Potential Maps of Canada, NRCan, 2023 data vintage). Québec provincial average: 1,150 kWh/kWp/year. Hydro-Québec residential Rate D (standard household rate, effective April 2024): approximately **7.3 ¢/kWh** (first 40 kWh/day tier) — lowest in North America.

**Supporting facts:**
- Hydro-Québec's grid: ~37,000 MW installed capacity; 99% renewable (hydroelectric + wind + biomass) as of 2022; GHG intensity approximately 0.6 g CO₂eq/kWh — among the lowest of any grid globally (Hydro-Québec Annual Report 2023; Canada Clean Electricity overview 2024).
- The Des Cantons substation at Windsor, Estrie is the planned terminus of Hydro-Québec's new 1,200 MW HVDC direct-current link to New Hampshire — confirming Estrie as a transmission hub, not a periphery, of the provincial grid.
- Hydro-Québec announced a 300 MW solar RFP in 2024 (first in its history) and an action plan to reach 3,000 MW solar by 2035 — indicating solar will play a growing role even in a hydro-dominant province.
- Provincial solar incentive: Hydro-Québec announced residential solar grant programs for 2026 expected to cover up to 40% of installation costs, which will meaningfully improve the economics of on-site generation.
- Wind energy expansion: 1,555 MW of new wind capacity tendered in January 2024 for delivery 2027–2029; long-term target of +10,000 MW wind by 2035 (Hydro-Québec, 2024).
- Electricity cost advantage is significant for energy-intensive operations: greenhouses, workshops, electric vehicle charging, heat-pump heating — all are dramatically cheaper in Estrie than in any US or European regen candidate region.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The energy picture for a regen settlement in Estrie is uniquely favourable: clean grid power at low cost eliminates most of the energy-poverty risk that burdens off-grid or high-tariff regions. The constraint is that grid dependency is real — the grid is not under community control, and Hydro-Québec's rate structure discourages net-metering by setting low export prices. A settlement seeking genuine energy sovereignty would need significant battery storage to decouple from the grid, which at current costs is hard to justify economically against 7 ¢/kWh grid power. The practical sweet spot is grid-connected with modest on-site solar + battery as resilience against outages, rather than full off-grid. Quebec's cold winters mean energy for heating is significant — heat pumps are the recommended transition technology, already economical at current rates and improving year by year.
