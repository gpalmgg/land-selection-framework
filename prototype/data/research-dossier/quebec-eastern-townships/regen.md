# Regenerative Network — Québec Eastern Townships (Estrie), Canada

**Headline finding:** The Eastern Townships carry a modest but genuine regenerative ecosystem. Within roughly 100 km of Sherbrooke, practitioners can identify approximately 8–12 intentional communities, ecovillages, permaculture projects, and aligned farms — concentrated along the Appalachian foothills and near Sherbrooke, Granby, Magog, and Lac-Mégantic. The provincial network is francophone-dominant and culturally distinct from Anglophone regen networks, which creates both coherence (shared values in the joie de vivre / slow-living tradition) and insularity (English-speaking founders face a real cultural integration challenge).

**Key data point (with vintage):** Approximate count of active intentional communities, ecovillages, and permaculture projects within ~100 km of Sherbrooke, drawn from the Répertoire des éco-communautés du Québec (2010, 36 entries province-wide), GEN Canada directory (2024), and direct site verification: approximately **8–12 active sites**, including La Cité Écologique (Ham-Nord, ~90 km, 100+ residents, founded 1984), Écohameau Estrie (Estrie region, permaculture training), Les Coteaux du Lac (Eastman, 20 ecological homes on 100 acres), and multiple smaller farms and intentional living projects.

**Supporting facts:**
- La Cité Écologique de Ham-Nord is described as the largest ecovillage in Québec, with ~100 residents, two accredited schools, six businesses, and an organic market farm (Bio-Maraîchère) offering internships. Founded 1984, it has operated through bankruptcy and renewal — demonstrating unusual institutional durability for the sector. Approximately 90–95 km north of Sherbrooke.
- Les Coteaux du Lac (Eastman, ~60 km from Sherbrooke): 20 ecological homes on 100 acres, with 55 acres stewarded by a local conservation society — one of the cleaner examples of ecological cohousing in the region.
- Écohameau Estrie: an ecohameau project offering permaculture training, focused on recreating "nourishing and harmonious ecosystems" — active as of 2023.
- Québec's first ecovillage directory (2010) listed 36 communities, eco-settlements, eco-projects, and sustainability-oriented rural towns province-wide. The concentration in the Estrie/Centre-du-Québec corridor reflects both land affordability and proximity to Montréal's urban regenerative population.
- GEN Canada was formally constituted in 2019; Québec's participation remains partial, with GEN Québec attempted but stalled at ~3–4 formally self-identifying écovillages.
- Facebook group "Écovillages et Éco-Communautés (Québec + Francophonie)" has active membership and serves as the informal coordination layer in lieu of a formal regional network.
- Permaculture Québec and the Réseau québécois pour la simplicité volontaire (RQSV) provide adjacent networks with overlapping membership — practitioners moving in these circles will find connections relatively quickly.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The regen network in Estrie is real but fragmented and francophone-dominated. A new project launched by non-francophones faces a genuine cultural integration challenge — the network runs in French, community norms are shaped by Québécois culture, and provincial bureaucracy (CPTAQ applications, municipal zoning variances) operates primarily in French. This is not insurmountable but it is a load-bearing factor that distinguishes this region from Anglophone Canada or comparable European networks. The flip side: the joie de vivre cultural substrate — artisan food, seasonal rhythms, hunting and maple traditions — has deep affinity with regen values, and Québec has a robust cooperative sector (cooperatives, CLTs, nonprofit land vehicles) that can support collective land-holding structures.
