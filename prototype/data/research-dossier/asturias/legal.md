# Legal + Economic — Asturias, Spain

**Headline finding:** Asturias operates under the same Spanish national legal regime as Galicia — no nationality restriction on land ownership; non-EU buyers acquire on equal terms with EU citizens via NIE registration. Like Galicia, Asturias is severely fragmented into smallholdings; unlike Galicia, the relevant collective-ownership tradition is *montes vecinales* and *parroquia rural* structures with similar but not identical legal form. The April 2025 end of Spain's Golden Visa applies but alternative residency routes remain. Cost-per-hectare in Asturias is slightly below Spain's national average.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Average agricultural land price in Asturias 2024: **€9,742/ha** (MAPA *Encuesta de Precios de la Tierra* 2024). Spain national average 2024: **€10,248/ha** (+2.8% YoY). Asturian breakdown by use type: permanent pasture €5,000–€10,000/ha, arable €10,000–€15,000/ha, *huerta* (irrigated horticulture) higher; mountain marginal land often €1,500–€4,000/ha. *Vintage note: Asturias-specific 2024 figure is an MAPA aggregated mean; underlying variance by valley and use-class is wide.*

**Supporting facts:**
- Foreign land ownership in Asturias / Spain: open, no nationality restriction (Ley 19/2003).
- Non-EU buyer path: NIE (Spanish foreign-identification number), Spanish bank account, notarised escritura, Registro de la Propiedad. Transactions >€500k reported to Banco de España (statistical, not approval). Same regime as Galicia.
- Spain Golden Visa (Ley 14/2013) repealed April 2025 for new applications; remaining residency pathways: non-lucrative visa (passive income), digital-nomad visa (Ley 28/2022), entrepreneurial visa.
- Asturian Estatuto de Autonomía devolves planning to the Principado; land use governed by **Decreto Legislativo 1/2004** (Texto Refundido de las disposiciones legales vigentes en materia de ordenación del territorio y urbanismo del Principado de Asturias) and amendments.
- Collective ownership: Sociedad Cooperativa Asturiana (Ley 4/2010 del Principado de Asturias de Cooperativas), SAT (Sociedad Agraria de Transformación), Asociación. *Montes vecinales / montes comunales* tradition exists but is less codified than Galician *comunidades de montes*.
- Construction permits on rural land go through *Ayuntamiento* (municipal) review under regional urbanism rules; *suelo no urbanizable* categories (especially of *protección agraria* and *forestal*) restrict residential construction.
- Anti-speculation: Spain has no equivalent of Romania's 8-year-resale tax; ordinary capital-gains taxes apply to land sales.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The defining Asturian practical reality is supply of abandoned and partially-abandoned aldeas (hamlets). Many are technically saleable but require detailed title-clean-up — descendants of pre-1970 emigrants to Buenos Aires, Mexico, or Cuba may still hold registered shares. Patient buyers can assemble surprisingly large parcels at very low total prices, but legal and notarial fees scale with complexity. Construction permitting around Biosphere Reserves and Natural Parks adds an additional layer; outside protected zones, *ayuntamientos* are generally responsive to repoblación projects (politically popular). Non-EU buyers face no land-purchase friction; the residency-acquisition path requires separate visa engineering.
