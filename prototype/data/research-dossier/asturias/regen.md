# Regenerative Ecosystem — Asturias, Spain

**Headline finding:** Asturias hosts a growing repoblación (re-population) ecosystem of abandoned-village revival projects — hamlets and *aldeas* across the interior valleys are being purchased and restored by individuals and cooperatives, with several long-running intentional-community examples. The regen practice is heavily tied to traditional Asturian smallholder culture (*aldeanos*) and to the *Reserva de la Biosfera* designations (six in Asturias, the highest density in Spain).

**Key data point (with vintage):** Asturias hosts **six UNESCO Biosphere Reserves** (Picos de Europa, Somiedo, Muniellos, Redes, Las Ubiñas-La Mesa, Río Eo Oscos y Terras de Burón) covering ~25% of the regional territory (UNESCO MAB + Principado de Asturias 2024). Asturias has **~333,000 ha of usable agricultural area**, of which **~94% is permanent pasture** (SADEI 2024). Active repoblación / village-revival projects mapped through 2024: 30+ initiatives in the regional network *Asturies en Acción* and parallel platforms.

**Supporting facts:**
- Repoblación projects: Aldea Nueva (Cangas de Onís area), Aldeasolar / various small communities in Valle del Nalón; SmartRural network of intentional-community projects across western Asturias.
- Sociedad Asturiana de Estudios Agroambientales y de la Biodiversidad — regional agroecology research/network.
- Coordinadora Ecoloxista d'Asturies — environmental coalition active in policy and direct restoration projects.
- Camín de la Tierra cooperative — agroecology training and producer cooperative.
- Active 2024–2026 funder/programme list (verified): **PEPAC España + Asturias regional CAP** (eco-schemes, agroforestry, mountain-area payments — Asturias receives high per-ha mountain-area payments), **PDR Asturias 2014–2022 + transition 2023–2027** administered by Consejería de Medio Rural, **Fondo de Reto Demográfico** (national programme funding rural-revitalisation), **MITECO Plan Estratégico de Patrimonio Natural** (national biodiversity strategy with regional allocations), **LEADER Asturias** (multiple Grupos de Acción Local — Comarca Oviedo, Camín Real de la Mesa, Oriente, etc.), **Fundación Biodiversidad** national grants.
- Picos de Europa National Park + 4 Natural Parks regulate land use across ~25% of regional area.
- Asturian cider (*sidra*) tradition supports thousands of smallholder apple orchards — recognised by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage 2024.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Asturias is the Spanish region where buying a whole abandoned aldea is a realistic proposition — 30-year decline has produced inventories of intact village clusters with running water and structural buildings at prices unimaginable in Western Europe. The trade-off: most are far from services, partially-titled (Galicia-like minifundio with diaspora heirs), and require integration with strongly independent local culture. UNESCO Biosphere Reserve overlays are practitioner-positive (eco-payment access) but add planning friction inside zones of highest ecological protection. EU and national funding is generous for traditional pasture management; less so for new-build community projects.
