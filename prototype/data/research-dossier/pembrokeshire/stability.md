# Political Stability — Pembrokeshire / West Wales, UK

**Headline finding:** The UK ranks high on institutional stability — but post-Brexit and post-2024 election the policy landscape relevant to non-UK buyers has shifted markedly. The July 2024 general election produced a Labour majority (PM Keir Starmer), ending 14 years of Conservative government; Wales has had a Labour-led Welsh Government continuously since 1999, with Eluned Morgan as First Minister since August 2024. Conflict risk is essentially nil; the relevant stability question is policy continuity around land, agriculture, and farm-inheritance tax.

**Key data point (with vintage):** V-Dem Electoral Democracy Index for United Kingdom 2023: **0.82** (V-Dem v14, March 2024 release) — declined from ~0.88 in 2010s; UK ranks below several smaller EU democracies on V-Dem electoral component but holds top-tier overall. UCDP conflict events in Pembrokeshire 2019–2024: **zero** (UCDP GED v24.1). INFORM Risk 2024 for United Kingdom: **2.5/10** (low risk; INFORM 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- The UK is a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy; Wales has devolved government since 1999 (Senedd Cymru) with extensive powers over planning, agriculture, environment, health, education.
- 4 July 2024 general election: Labour 411 seats, Conservatives 121, Liberal Democrats 72, SNP 9, Reform UK 5, Plaid Cymru 4. Wales returned 27 Labour MPs out of 32. Mandate runs to 2029.
- Welsh Government: Labour minority backed by 2024 cooperation arrangement with Plaid Cymru; Eluned Morgan succeeded Vaughan Gething August 2024.
- Civil-society infrastructure in West Wales is dense: Wales Council for Voluntary Action, PLANED (Pembrokeshire Local Action Network for Enterprise and Development), Pembrokeshire Coast National Park Authority, Coed Cymru, Tir Coed.
- Brexit took effect 31 January 2020 / end of transition 31 December 2020 — separated UK from EU Common Agricultural Policy and free-movement; post-Brexit agricultural policy in Wales now runs through the Sustainable Farming Scheme (consultation 2024, expected launch 2025–2026).
- Inheritance Tax Agricultural Property Relief was capped at £1m in the November 2024 Budget (Labour), to take effect April 2026 — a meaningful policy shock for family-farm succession planning.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Pembrokeshire's stability environment is solid but operates inside a UK policy regime in transition. The 2024 IHT cap on Agricultural Property Relief is one of the most consequential land-policy shifts in a generation and is reshaping the market — expect more farm sales 2025–2027 as families restructure. The Welsh devolution dimension matters: planning, agriculture and environment are Cardiff-controlled, not Westminster — which has produced policies (One Planet Development, see legal.md) that exist nowhere else in the UK.
