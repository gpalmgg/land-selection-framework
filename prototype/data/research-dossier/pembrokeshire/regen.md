# Regenerative Ecosystem — Pembrokeshire / West Wales, UK

**Headline finding:** West Wales is one of the most active regen-and-low-impact-living jurisdictions in the UK, anchored by the Welsh Government's One Planet Development policy (TAN 6) — the only planning framework in Europe explicitly designed to permit one-family low-impact rural smallholdings. Lammas in Glandwr (Pembrokeshire) is the iconic OPD ecovillage. The Centre for Alternative Technology (CAT, Powys) is a 50-year-old regen-education anchor for the wider region.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Cumulative One Planet Development approvals in Wales 2011–2024: **~50 approved sites** (Welsh Government data, last fully published 2019 with subsequent partial updates; figure consistent with One Planet Council practitioner tracking through 2024). Lammas (Glandwr) hosts ~9 ecological smallholdings across ~76 acres (Lammas website 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- Lammas Ecovillage (Glandwr, Pembrokeshire) — pioneering 2009 One Planet Development site, multi-household low-impact community; documented planning case study.
- Centre for Alternative Technology (CAT, Machynlleth) — long-running regen-education institution founded 1973; graduate programmes in sustainable architecture and ecology.
- Down to Earth (Murton, Gower) — regen-skills and ecological-design training.
- Pembrokeshire-specific projects: Tir Coed (woodland-skills NGO), Pembrokeshire Coast National Park's farming-in-protected-landscapes scheme, several smaller OPD applications and approvals in the county through 2020–2024.
- Active 2024–2026 funder/programme list (verified): **Sustainable Farming Scheme (SFS)** — Welsh Government's post-Brexit agri-environment scheme (consultation 2024; rollout 2025–2026), **Glastir successor / Habitat Wales Scheme** (interim 2024 measure), **Welsh Government Rural Communities Development Programme** (LEADER successor), **PLANED** (Pembrokeshire LAG), **National Lottery Community Fund Wales** (regular grant calls), **Natural Resources Wales — Nature Networks Fund**, **WWF-Cymru regen partnerships**.
- One Planet Council operates as the practitioner-led peer-support network for OPD applicants and residents.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
West Wales is uniquely friendly to small-scale low-impact projects because OPD/TAN 6 explicitly carves out planning permission for ecological smallholdings that meet a strict management plan including 65% household subsistence from the land within 5 years. The catch is that OPD is demanding: it requires a detailed management plan, ecological footprint calculations, and annual monitoring — many applicants underestimate the commitment. The wider regen-funder ecosystem is in transition post-Brexit; the SFS rollout (2025–2026) is the dominant 2024–2026 policy uncertainty. Foreign (non-UK) applicants face no additional regen-funding barriers but UK tax-residency materially affects most grants.
