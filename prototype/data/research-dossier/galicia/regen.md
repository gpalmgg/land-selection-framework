# Regenerative Ecosystem — Galicia, Spain

**Headline finding:** Galicia's regen ecosystem is shaped by two distinctive features — the *montes vecinales* commons (~600,000 ha of community-owned forest and grazing) and a wave of *neorrurales* (urban-to-rural returners) repopulating abandoned hamlets (*aldeas*). The result is a working substrate for cooperative agroecology and aldea-revival projects, with thinner but growing EU-financed funding lines.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Global Ecovillage Network entries in Galicia & nearby (Lakabe over the border in Navarra is iconic but outside Galicia; Galicia-proper entries Matavenero is in León — Galicia itself has fewer GEN-listed sites): **~3–5 active community/regen entries** in Galicia accessed via ecovillage.org and Red Ibérica de Ecoaldeas, 2025. *Montes vecinales en man común* cover **~600,000 ha — ~22% of Galicia's land area** (Xunta de Galicia 2024 figure).

**Supporting facts:**
- Red Ibérica de Ecoaldeas (RIE) — the Iberian ecovillage network — maintains the most complete Galicia-aware directory; member projects include Arterra Bizimodu (Navarra), Lakabe, and several Galicia-based cooperatives in transition.
- Agroecology / regen-aligned actors active in Galicia: Sindicato Labrego Galego (small-farmer union), FRUGA (rural farmers' federation), Mercado da Cosecha network, and Mariscadoras de Lourizán (regen shellfish cooperative).
- Aldea-revival projects: Aldeas Abandonadas / Galician Village Project, Proxecto Aldeas Modelo (Xunta initiative since 2020), regen-tourism cooperatives in Ribeira Sacra and the Sil canyon.
- Active 2024–2026 funder/programme list (verified): **PEPAC España / Galicia CAP Strategic Plan 2023–2027** (Xunta-administered eco-schemes), **AGADER** (Galician rural-development agency, LEADER 2023–2027 local-action groups), **Fundación Biodiversidad** (national grants for ecological restoration), **Programa Aldeas Modelo** (Xunta funding for abandoned-village restoration & forest fire-prevention silvopastoral conversion), **ICO Verde** loans (Spain's official credit institute green-finance line).
- Forest-fire risk shapes the regen agenda — silvopastoral systems and *xestión forestal sostible* are policy priorities and where most regen money flows.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Galicia is a regen jurisdiction where the *commons* layer matters more than the ecovillage layer. Most viable community projects engage with existing *comunidades de montes* rather than try to assemble freehold parcels. The Aldeas Modelo programme is genuinely useful for international groups willing to operate in Galego and partner with a comunidade de montes — but it requires a Spanish legal entity. AGADER LEADER funding is the most accessible regional grant for foreign-led projects with a Galician partner.
