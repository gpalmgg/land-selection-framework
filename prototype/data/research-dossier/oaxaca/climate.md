# Climate — Oaxaca highlands, Mexico

**Headline finding:** The Oaxaca highlands sit in a rare highland-tropical thermal band: Oaxaca City (1,550 m) records a current mean annual temperature of ~18.5°C, while the Sierra Norte above 2,000 m is several degrees cooler. Under SSP2-4.5, CMIP6 ensemble modelling projects a warming of +1.5–2.0°C across south-southeast Mexico by 2041–2060 relative to the 1980–2014 ERA5 baseline, pushing the Valles Centrales toward ~20–20.5°C MAT and keeping the Sierra Norte comfortably below 18°C. The region sits at the boundary of the framework's 25°C flag threshold and is not expected to breach it at elevation; the lowland Pacific Oaxaca coast is a different story.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Mean annual temperature projection 2041–2060 under SSP2-4.5: **~20.0–20.5°C** for Valles Centrales / Oaxaca City; **~16–18°C** for Sierra Norte above 2,000 m (derived from CMIP6 four-model ensemble — CNRM-ESM2-1, IPSL-CM6A-LR, MIROC6, MRI-ESM2-0 — with ERA5 bias correction, 0.25° resolution; Vázquez-Aguirre et al., Climate 2023; consistent with World Bank CCKP mean projections for southern Mexico). Current baseline (WorldClim/ERA5 1970–2000): ~18.5°C at Oaxaca City.

**Supporting facts:**
- Oaxaca and Guerrero are identified as the states with the most municipalities having high exposure to current climate change conditions in Mexico (World Bank Climate Change Knowledge Portal, 2024).
- South-southeast Mexico warming under SSP2-4.5 for 2041–2060 projects a mean increase of +1.5°C relative to 1980–2014 ERA5 baseline, with dry-season (March–May) temperatures warming fastest (+1.8°C) (Vázquez-Aguirre et al., MDPI Climate 2023).
- Maximum temperature of warmest month (BIO5) for Oaxaca City highlands: currently ~32°C; projected to reach ~33.5–34°C by 2050 — hot but within adaptive range given diurnal cooling at altitude.
- The Sierra Norte cloud forest zone experiences night frost at elevations above 2,500 m; this frost window may narrow but not disappear by 2050 under moderate-emissions scenarios.
- Annual precipitation over the Oaxaca highlands averages 700–1,000 mm, bimodal, with a dry season November–April. Climate projections indicate increased variability and a shift toward more intense rainfall events.
- On a high-carbon pathway (SSP5-8.5), warming in Mexico could reach +2.1°C by 2050 at national scale; SSP2-4.5 is the more relevant planning scenario and yields lower bounds.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The critical distinction in Oaxaca is altitude band. A settlement sited in the Valles Centrales at 1,500–1,700 m faces a mid-century climate that is warm but workable, analogous to a warm-Mediterranean microclimate with a genuine dry season requiring storage infrastructure. A settlement in the Sierra Norte at 1,800–2,500 m gains 2–4°C of thermal buffer and remains temperate through 2050 under SSP2-4.5 — arguably the most climate-resilient band in the entire North American regen-community landscape. The real climate risk in Oaxaca is not temperature but water seasonality: an intensifying dry season means passive earthworks (swales, tanks, cloud-forest fog harvest) are not optional. Practitioners should treat altitude as the primary site-selection variable before evaluating any other criterion.
