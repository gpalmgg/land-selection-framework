# Energy — Oaxaca highlands, Mexico

**Headline finding:** Oaxaca is one of the most solar-rich states in the Americas. The highland plateau and Valles Centrales receive exceptional solar irradiance year-round, and Mexico's national average specific PV yield of ~1,800 kWh/kWp/yr is exceeded in the Oaxaca highlands. This exceeds the upper bound of the framework's EU comparison range (800–2,000 kWh/kWp/yr) and should be flagged accordingly. Off-grid solar is genuinely economical here even without grid connection, which is a material advantage in communities located in areas where grid infrastructure is unreliable or absent.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Global Solar Atlas v2.7, specific PV yield (PVOUT) for the Oaxaca highlands region: **approximately 1,900–2,100 kWh/kWp/yr** (consistent with Global Solar Atlas country data for Mexico published by the World Bank; Mexico national average ~1,800 kWh/kWp/yr; Oaxaca highland plateau benchmarked above national average based on GHI data in the 5.5–6.0 kWh/m²/day range reported for the region). *Direct portal query at the centroid coordinate [−96.7, 17.1] was not completed in this research pipeline; the value is derived from published national data and Mexico solar maps. Confidence: medium. Direct verification via globalsolaratlas.info interactive tool is recommended.*

**Supporting facts:**
- Mexico's national average PV yield ~1,800 kWh/kWp/yr; high-altitude and high-irradiance zones (Central Plateau, Pacific coast slopes) regularly yield above 2,000 kWh/kWp/yr (Energy Tracker Asia; Mexico Energy Partners, 2024).
- Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) for Oaxaca state falls in the 5.5–6.3 kWh/m²/day band according to World Bank Mexico solar maps — towards the high end of the national range.
- Mexico's installed solar capacity grew rapidly 2022–2025; Oaxaca's Isthmus of Tehuantepec region (adjacent to study area) is already the site of one of the world's largest wind+solar hybrid zones, confirming the renewable energy potential.
- Grid connectivity in the Sierra Norte and rural Valles Centrales is present but unreliable; communities in the Sierra Norte have documented experience with off-grid and micro-grid operation.
- Average daily solar hours in Oaxaca: ~6.5–7 hours of peak sun; the dry season (November–April) provides particularly consistent irradiance.
- The Isthmus wind corridor (Tehuantepec) begins approximately 100–150 km south of the research centroid; micro-wind is feasible on exposed ridgelines in the Sierra Norte above 2,000 m.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Solar energy is arguably the easiest infrastructure win in the Oaxaca highlands — almost uniquely so in the global regen-community landscape. A well-sited 5 kWp system will produce 9,500–10,500 kWh/yr with good seasonal consistency, making electrification independent of Mexico's CFE (Federal Electricity Commission) grid realistic from day one. The more important energy question for practitioners is not generation but storage and appliance discipline: cloudy periods during the June–September rainy season reduce daily output, so battery sizing should account for 3–5 consecutive low-irradiance days. The community-scale pattern that works well in Oaxaca's highlands is hybrid micro-grid (solar + battery + small diesel backup for extended cloudiness) serving 10–30 households, with surplus sold or used for water pumping.
