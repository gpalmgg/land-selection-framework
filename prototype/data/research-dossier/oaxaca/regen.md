# Regen Network — Oaxaca highlands, Mexico

**Headline finding:** Oaxaca is one of the most densely networked agroecology and regenerative-practice regions in the Americas. The highland landscape hosts a thick layer of indigenous agroecological knowledge (Zapotec and Mixtec milpa systems, traditional forest management), active practitioner organisations (CEDICAM, Alternativa Agrícola Suchixtepec, Tierra del Sol), intentional communities (Komunidad Kolinda), and an internationally connected network of permaculture and ecovillage projects accessible via GEN Latin America / CASA Mexico. This density is an exceptional asset compared to any European region in this framework.

**Key data point (with vintage):** GEN directory + OpenStreetMap + NuMundo survey count of intentional-community / permaculture / ecovillage / agroecology network sites within ~100 km of Oaxaca City: **estimated 8–15 identifiable sites** (Komunidad Kolinda [GEN registered, Sierra Sur, 2018–present], Tierra del Sol [Valles Centrales, active], Alternativa Agrícola Suchixtepec [Sierra Sur, WWF-supported], CEDICAM network [Sierra Mixteca, Goldman Prize 2008], Micologica [Sierra Norte], Sembrando Vida Ecoaldea [NuMundo listed], plus multiple OSM-mapped organic farms and permaculture demonstration sites). *A systematic GEN + OSM + NuMundo count was not completed in this pipeline; this figure reflects named sites found in research. The true count is likely higher given the density of informal indigenous agroecology. Confidence: medium.*

**Supporting facts:**
- **Komunidad Kolinda**: GEN-registered intentional ecovillage, 6.5 ha of forested shared land at 2,500 m elevation in the Sierra Sur, 11 international permanent residents, founded 2018. Practices: regenerative agriculture, bioconstruction, traditional medicine, Zapotec cultural alignment, permaculture workshops. (GEN, 2024; kolinda.oaxacasierrasur.com)
- **Tierra del Sol**: Permaculture demonstration farm 20 km from Oaxaca City in the Valles Centrales. Runs agroecology workshops for local farmers and international learners. (Circular Economy for Food, 2023; NuMundo)
- **Micologica**: Community mushroom enterprise in the Sierra Norte. Low-tech, organic, closed-loop cultivation. Internationally documented model.
- **CEDICAM** (Centro de Desarrollo Integral Campesino de la Mixteca Alta): farmer-to-farmer agroecology network active since 1997. Goldman Environmental Prize 2008. Documented soil and forest rehabilitation across the Mixteca Alta. Not an ecovillage but one of Mexico's most cited regenerative agriculture practice networks.
- **Alternativa Agrícola Suchixtepec**: Sierra Sur community enterprise with WWF support; agroecological coffee and food systems within indigenous territory.
- **GEN Latin America / CASA Mexico** directory lists multiple Mexican intentional communities; several operate in Oaxaca's broader region.
- **NuMundo** platform lists Oaxaca-region centres for permaculture, plant medicine, and regenerative learning — a distinct network from GEN with partial geographic overlap.
- **Indigenous context**: Oaxaca's 418 usos-y-costumbres municipalities maintain traditional milpa, agroforestry, and communal forest management systems that pre-date and outperform most "regen" frameworks on measurable biodiversity and carbon metrics.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The regen network in Oaxaca is simultaneously unusually rich and unusually indigenous-embedded. An incoming international community has genuine peer communities to learn from and potentially collaborate with — but the primary carriers of regenerative knowledge are Zapotec, Mixtec, and Chatino communities who have managed this landscape for generations. The most successful international settlements in the region (Komunidad Kolinda being the clearest example) have grounded themselves explicitly in relationship with local indigenous neighbours rather than arriving as independent "regen projects." Practitioners who treat the indigenous knowledge system as peripheral or secondary will miss the region's most durable asset and likely generate community friction. The relational work is not optional here.
