# Accessibility + Demographics — Saxony-Anhalt, Germany

**Headline finding:** Saxony-Anhalt offers strong German baseline infrastructure (autobahn coverage, ICE rail nodes at Halle and Magdeburg, hospital and school networks intact) combined with the EU's steepest rural-depopulation trajectory in non-Mediterranean Europe. Berlin is ~1.5 hours, Hamburg ~3 hours, Leipzig ~1 hour. Internet rollout is good in cities, uneven in remote rural areas. Demographics: one of Germany's oldest median ages and continuous population decline.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Saxony-Anhalt population 2024: **~2.16 million** across ~20,450 km² → **~106 inhabitants/km²** (Statistisches Landesamt Sachsen-Anhalt 2024). Rural Landkreis density (e.g. Altmarkkreis Salzwedel): **~32/km²** (LSA 2024). Median age 2024: **~48 years** — among Germany's oldest (Destatis 2024). Population 2011→2023: **−4.5%** (LSA 2024); continued decline projected.

**Supporting facts:**
- Tertiary hospitals: Universitätsklinikum Magdeburg, Universitätsklinikum Halle (Saale), and a network of Kreiskrankenhäuser in most Landkreise. Rural settlements typically within 30–60 km of a hospital.
- Airports: Leipzig/Halle (LEJ) is the regional hub; Berlin Brandenburg (BER) ~1.5 h from eastern Saxony-Anhalt; Hannover (HAJ) accessible from western edge.
- Rail: ICE high-speed rail through Halle and Magdeburg; regional networks via Deutsche Bahn and private operators (Abellio formerly, replaced 2022 by various operators); Altmark and Harz are less well-served by rail.
- Road density: Autobahn A2 (Berlin–Ruhr), A9 (Berlin–Munich via Halle), A14 (Magdeburg–Dresden/Leipzig), A38 — Saxony-Anhalt is well-connected by motorway. Secondary roads good.
- Internet coverage: Germany's overall FTTH rollout lags EU peers; rural Saxony-Anhalt remains spotty. State broadband programme 2021–2025 aims for 75%+ FTTH coverage by 2025 (Breitbandatlas 2024).
- Electrification: 100% grid; Saxony-Anhalt is a major wind-energy producer; eastern-German microgeneration regime under EEG 2023.
- Demographic trend: continuous net out-migration since 1990; modest stabilisation post-2015 in metropolitan Halle/Magdeburg; rural depopulation continues; foreign-resident share remains low (~7%) but growing.
- Schools: rural school consolidation has reduced primary-school density; many Landkreise operate consolidated *Gemeinschaftsschulen*; *freie Schulen* (free/alternative schools) growing slowly — Waldorf and Montessori options present in larger towns.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Saxony-Anhalt's accessibility geography combines strong urban-corridor infrastructure (Halle–Magdeburg–Berlin axis) with deep depopulation in the Altmark and Harz fringes. For practitioners, this is a buy-low / commute-out pattern: cheap land in the northern Altmark, with ICE-accessible cities 1–2 h away. Internet quality is the most frequent practitioner complaint — confirm fibre or LTE-Advanced coverage at parcel level via Breitbandatlas. The German education system runs largely in German; foreign families integrating need to commit to German fluency early. Healthcare quality at university clinics is excellent; rural Hausarzt practices increasingly understaffed.
