# Legal + Economic — Saxony-Anhalt, Germany

**Headline finding:** Germany places no nationality restriction on freehold land ownership — non-EU and EU buyers acquire on equal terms with no residency precondition. The key Saxony-Anhalt feature is the **agricultural-land transfer regime (Grundstücksverkehrsgesetz, GrdstVG) and the Landpachtverkehrsgesetz**, which give regional Landgesellschaften and the state pre-emption priority for agricultural-land sales to favour active farmers. The BVVG (federal agency winding down ex-GDR state land) has historically been a major seller and is approaching closure (Bundestag decision 2018 to wind down by 2030). Saxony-Anhalt land is among Germany's most affordable.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Average purchase price for *Ackerland* (arable land) in Saxony-Anhalt 2024: **€22,022/ha** (agrarheute / Bauernzeitung 2024 analysis citing state statistics office). Average *Bodenrichtwert* (standard reference value) for arable land 2024: **€2.00/m² = €20,000/ha**, stable YoY (Gutachterausschuss Sachsen-Anhalt 2024). Magdeburger Börde top-quality black-earth parcels: substantially higher than state average. Average rental price (Pacht) 2024: **€429/ha** (state agricultural ministry 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- Germany's land ownership regime: no nationality restriction; Grundbuch (land register) registration via Notar. Costs include 3.5% Grunderwerbsteuer in Saxony-Anhalt + 1.5–2.5% notary/registry fees.
- **Grundstücksverkehrsgesetz (GrdstVG)** — 1961 federal law: agricultural-land sales above ~1 ha require approval from the *Landwirtschaftsbehörde*; the authority may refuse if the buyer is not an active farmer or if a farmer is prepared to buy at the same price (essentially a pre-emption mechanism).
- **Landgesellschaft Sachsen-Anhalt** — semi-public entity that exercises pre-emption to support active farmers; the practical effect is that pure-investor land purchases are routinely blocked or rerouted.
- **BVVG (Bodenverwertungs- und -verwaltungs GmbH)** — federal agency selling ex-GDR state-owned agricultural land; in wind-down phase per 2018 Bundestag decision; remaining sales continue 2024–2030.
- Foreign buyers face no specific legal barrier but must satisfy the same active-farmer / land-use criteria as domestic non-farmer buyers; standard workaround is incorporation of a German GbR / GmbH agricultural entity with farmer management.
- No Golden Visa equivalent in Germany; residency routes include Blue Card (skilled work), self-employment under §21 AufenthG, freelance under §21(5) AufenthG, family reunification.
- Collective-ownership structures: GbR (Gesellschaft bürgerlichen Rechts), GmbH (limited company), eingetragene Genossenschaft (cooperative), Stiftung (foundation), e.V. (registered association). Sieben Linden operates as a Genossenschaft with associated e.V. for community functions.
- Construction permits on rural land via Bauordnungsrecht (state) and Baugesetzbuch (federal) — *Außenbereich* (open countryside) construction is severely restricted; §35 BauGB *privilegierte Vorhaben* clause is the standard path for farm-related buildings.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The biggest practitioner surprise in Germany is the active-farmer-preference regime — the right to buy agricultural land is conditional on land-use intent. Community projects without an agricultural plan or partnership often find purchases blocked at the Landwirtschaftsbehörde stage. The standard workaround: incorporate an agricultural Genossenschaft or GmbH with a farmer-shareholder, prepare a written land-use concept (Nutzungskonzept), and engage the Landgesellschaft early. BVVG closures may concentrate land disposal in 2024–2028 — opportunity for projects positioned to acquire. Saxony-Anhalt black-earth land in the Magdeburger Börde commands premium prices and faces stiff farmer competition; northern Altmark and Harz fringes are more accessible.
