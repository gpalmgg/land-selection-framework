# Political Stability — Saxony-Anhalt, Germany

**Headline finding:** Germany is one of the EU's most institutionally stable democracies — V-Dem top decile, INFORM Risk in the lowest band. The 23 February 2025 federal election produced a CDU/CSU + SPD grand coalition under Chancellor Friedrich Merz (sworn in May 2025). Saxony-Anhalt has had stable CDU-led state government for over a decade (Minister-President Reiner Haseloff since 2011). The relevant stability concern is the steady rise of the AfD: in Saxony-Anhalt, the AfD polled ~25% in the 2024 European Parliament vote and is the largest opposition party in the Landtag.

**Key data point (with vintage):** V-Dem Electoral Democracy Index for Germany 2023: **0.86** (V-Dem v14, March 2024 release; top decile globally). UCDP conflict events in Saxony-Anhalt 2019–2024: **zero** (UCDP GED v24.1). INFORM Risk 2024 for Germany: **1.9/10** (very low risk; INFORM 2024). AfD vote share in Saxony-Anhalt 2024 European Parliament election: **~25%** (Landeswahlleiter 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- Germany operates under the 1949 Grundgesetz with federal-state (Bund-Länder) division of competence. Saxony-Anhalt is one of 16 Länder, with extensive devolved powers over education, planning, agriculture (within Bund/EU framework), police, culture.
- Federal political cycle 2024–2025: Olaf Scholz's SPD-Greens-FDP coalition collapsed November 2024 when Scholz dismissed FDP finance minister Lindner; federal elections held February 2025; CDU/CSU under Merz won, formed coalition with SPD; Greens and FDP excluded from government.
- Saxony-Anhalt Landtag (state parliament): CDU + SPD + FDP coalition under Haseloff (re-elected 2021); next state elections 2026.
- AfD is classified as a *Verdachtsfall* (suspected case) for monitoring by the Verfassungsschutz nationally (since 2021) and in Saxony-Anhalt was confirmed as right-wing extremist (Landesverfassungsschutz Sachsen-Anhalt assessment 2023). This is a meaningful institutional response, not just rhetoric.
- Civil-society infrastructure: dense by EU standards — Caritas, Diakonie, AWO welfare networks; environmental NGOs (BUND, NABU) active in rural Saxony-Anhalt; agricultural producer cooperatives (former LPG-successor structures) significant in eastern German land economy.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
For a 50-year community project, Germany's institutional stability remains very high — the practical political-risk vector is the AfD trajectory in eastern Länder and the polarisation that follows. In rural Saxony-Anhalt, the AfD has secured plurality positions in several Kreistage (district councils); this does not threaten property or contractual rights but does change the social texture of village politics. Newcomer integration in rural eastern Germany often takes longer than in the south or west; foreign-led projects benefit from establishing visible local-economic contributions early. The Bund-Länder devolution means planning, agricultural permits, and infrastructure investment flow through Magdeburg, not Berlin.
