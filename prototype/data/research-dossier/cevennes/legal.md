# Legal + Economic — Cévennes / Languedoc, France

**Headline finding:** France places no nationality restriction on rural-land ownership — non-EU and EU citizens may purchase agricultural land on equal terms. The dominant practitioner constraint is SAFER (Société d'Aménagement Foncier et d'Établissement Rural), which holds **pre-emption rights on virtually every agricultural sale** above ~0.7 ha. SAFER does not block sales but can substitute itself for the buyer to reassign land to a young farmer or installation project. Cévennes land prices are extremely low by Western European standards.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Agricultural land prices 2024 in the Cévennes catchment: **Lozère €5,480/ha** (+8.1% YoY; SAFER 2024); **Gard €13,240/ha** (irrigated/wine-zone weight; SAFER 2024); **Hérault €7,900/ha** (SAFER 2024); **Aude €6,360/ha** (SAFER 2024); **Ardèche** in 2023 averaged ~**€5,500–€7,000/ha**, 2024 SAFER update expected similar (SAFER Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes). Occitanie regional average for free (non-rented) land 2024: **€7,280/ha**. SAFER notes "very strong growth in the Cévennes and Causses". Prices are published annually by SAFER + Agreste in *Barème indicatif de la valeur vénale moyenne des terres agricoles*.

**Supporting facts:**
- No nationality restriction on rural-land ownership in France — Code Rural et de la Pêche Maritime, Articles L143-1 et seq. (SAFER), Articles L411-1 et seq. (tenancy law).
- SAFER pre-emption: applies to most agricultural-land sales (>~70 ares / 0.7 ha) for 2 months from notification of sale; SAFER may substitute itself as buyer to reassign to a young farmer, installation project, or environmental purpose. This is the dominant Cévennes-specific legal feature.
- Non-EU buyers face additional administrative steps: notarised purchase via French notaire, French-resident bank account, fiscal-representative arrangement, but no ownership-restriction barrier (unlike Romania).
- French residency-from-purchase pathways: France has no Golden Visa equivalent; passive-income/talent (Passeport Talent), entrepreneur (VLS-TS Entrepreneur), and Visiteur (long-stay non-working) visas are the standard routes. None confer automatic land-purchase rights; conversely landownership does not confer residency.
- Collective-ownership structures: **GFA (Groupement Foncier Agricole)** — most common collective land-ownership vehicle for community-scale projects; **GAEC** (Groupement Agricole d'Exploitation en Commun) for joint farming; **Association Loi 1901** for non-commercial community organisation; **SCI** for general-purpose real-estate holding; **Foncière** (Terre de Liens model) for ethical land trusts.
- Terre de Liens is the major French ethical-land trust — has acquired ~350 farms by 2024 and rents to organic/regen farmers under bail rural environnemental.
- Parc National des Cévennes: Heart-zone (zone cœur) has additional construction and activity restrictions; Adhesion-zone (aire d'adhésion) lighter regime. Verify zoning before purchase.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The single most surprising constraint for foreign buyers is SAFER pre-emption. The practical workaround is constructive: SAFER officers often welcome regen/installation-project buyers, particularly those replacing existing farmers. Engaging the départemental SAFER office *before* signing a compromis de vente, presenting an installation project, often pre-empts pre-emption use. Within the Cévennes National Park heart-zone, additional restrictions on construction, livestock numbers, and access roads apply — read the park's *Charte* before committing. GFA structures and Terre de Liens partnerships are the most pre-tested vehicles for community-scale land in the Cévennes.
