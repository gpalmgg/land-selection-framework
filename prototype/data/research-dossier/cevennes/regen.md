# Regenerative Ecosystem — Cévennes / Languedoc, France

**Headline finding:** The Cévennes hold one of Europe's densest concentrations of post-1968 "néo-rural" communities, permaculture practitioners, and alternative-agriculture experiments. La Bergerie de Berdine, Longo Maï, Terre & Humanisme (founded by Pierre Rabhi), and the network around Movement Colibris constitute a 50-year-deep regen-cultural substrate. EU CAP funding flows through France's national plan; the Cévennes National Park and Biosphere Reserve status unlock specific eco-scheme payments.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Eurotopia and GEN France directory entries within Cévennes / Languedoc-Roussillon (Gard, Lozère, Ardèche, Hérault): **~15–25 active community/regen entries** (Eurotopia.de 2024 edition; GEN Europe 2024). Terre & Humanisme operates from Ardèche with 30+ years of permaculture-Africa-Mediterranean projects. Parc National des Cévennes designated UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 1985; covers ~2,930 km² overlapping Lozère and Gard.

**Supporting facts:**
- Terre & Humanisme (Ardèche, founded by Pierre Rabhi 1994; Rabhi died 2021) — agroecology training, permaculture in Mediterranean climates, North-South solidarity programmes.
- Longo Maï network — multi-site European cooperative founded 1973, with strong Provence/Cévennes presence; Le Pigeonnier (Limans) is foundational site.
- Mouvement Colibris (Pierre Rabhi-inspired, founded 2007) — distributed regen-association network with active Cévennes nodes; transitioning post-founder.
- La Bergerie de Berdine (Vaucluse — edge of Cévennes catchment) — therapeutic-community model with regen-agriculture practice.
- L'Université Populaire de Permaculture (UPP) operates training across France with strong Cévennes membership.
- Active 2024–2026 funder/programme list (verified): **PSN France (Plan Stratégique National)** — France CAP Strategic Plan 2023–2027 with ~€44bn envelope; eco-schemes, MAEC (mesures agro-environnementales et climatiques), Bio (organic) payments, **Agence de l'Eau Rhône-Méditerranée-Corse** (water-agency grants for ecological restoration), **ADEME** (French ecological-transition agency, regular regen-finance calls), **Fondation Lemarchand**, **Fondation de France**, **Région Occitanie Pacte Vert** (regional regen-finance line), **Fondation Daniel et Nina Carasso** (Mediterranean food-systems funder).
- Cévennes National Park has dedicated eco-pastoral budget supporting traditional transhumance, dry-stone walling, chestnut-orchard restoration.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The Cévennes regen scene is mature, networked, and somewhat tribal — 50 years of intentional-community life has produced multiple competing schools of practice (Colibris/Rabhi-aligned, Longo-Maï anarchist-cooperative, biodynamic Demeter-network, etc.). Newcomers benefit from this density but should expect cultural integration to require time and language. French CAP payments are administratively heavy and require French-language paperwork and a SIRET (business registration); foreign projects typically incorporate a French association or GAEC (agricultural partnership) for access. The Parc National's pastoral budget is the most distinctive regional regen-funding line and is genuinely useful for traditional-management projects.
