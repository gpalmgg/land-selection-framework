# Regen Network — Nova Scotia / Cape Breton, Canada

**Headline finding:** Nova Scotia hosts a small but real cluster of intentional communities, cohousing projects, and permaculture initiatives — mostly concentrated on the south shore and in the Annapolis Valley, with Cape Breton Island containing at least one active community. Within a ~100 km radius of most mainland Nova Scotia sites, 3–6 identifiable regen-adjacent projects can be located in the GEN, Foundation for Intentional Community (FIC), and Canadian Cohousing Network (CCN) directories, plus a broader informal permaculture and organic-farming network embedded in institutions like Perennia and the NS Organic Growers Association. This is a genuine but thin scene — more comparable to Ireland's west-coast situation than to the denser ecovillage geographies of central Europe or British Columbia.

**Key data point (with vintage):** GEN directory + Foundation for Intentional Community (FIC) + Canadian Cohousing Network (CCN) + Permaculture Global Network, communities within ~100 km of central Nova Scotia (Bridgewater / Halifax hub): **4–6 identifiable active or aspiring projects** (accessed 2025; includes Treehouse Village Ecohousing, Natura Regenerative Community, Eliopoli Ecovillage Project, Space of Love Cape Breton, plus several informal permaculture farms with work-exchange listings).

**Supporting facts:**
- **Treehouse Village Ecohousing** (Bridgewater, NS, 16 acres): Atlantic Canada's first cohousing community, 30 households now established and operational; listed in Canadian Cohousing Network and FIC directories; founded ~2018–2020. Confirmed operational.
- **Natura Regenerative Community** (Bridgewater, NS, 30 acres): aspiring ecovillage planning 16 residential units, shared community kitchen, greenhouse, workshop; listed in GEN directory; planning began 2013 with a 2018 target start — as of 2022–2025 the community's operational status is uncertain (comments on GEN page indicate development delays).
- **Eliopoli Ecovillage Project** (southwestern Nova Scotia): intentional community named "City of the Sun"; listed in Permaculture Global Network; size and operational status uncertain as of 2024.
- **Space of Love Community** (Cape Breton Island): listed community practising subsistence agriculture (vegetables, orchard, root cellar, greenhouse); small-scale, informal.
- **Intentional Communities Nova Scotia** (Facebook group, ~346 members, 2024): active social node connecting people interested in co-living and land projects across the province.
- **Perennia Food and Agriculture** (based in Truro): provincial agency supporting sustainable and organic farming with training, soil testing, and farm business support — de facto infrastructure node for new farmers.
- **Nova Scotia Organic Growers Association (NSOGA)**: active network of ~200+ organic growers; annual conference; informal peer-learning and land-access connections.
- GEN Canada (Ecovillages Canada) was formed in 2019 and serves as the national-level network; Nova Scotia participants are listed but the network is nascent compared to GEN Europe.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
The Nova Scotia regen scene is at an earlier stage than the clusters around Findhorn (Scotland), La Borie Noble (France), or the Finca Bellavista network — but it is not absent. The more interesting infrastructure for a new project may be the province's active alternative-agriculture community: NSOGA, Perennia, TapRoot Farms, and a cluster of market gardens and CSA farms in the Annapolis Valley and South Shore create practical knowledge transfer about what actually grows in the climate, what markets exist, and where to source labour. Cape Breton specifically has an underappreciated counter-cultural history (the Cabot Trail arts community, Gaelic cultural revival, back-to-the-landers from the 1970s) that gives it a particular texture of community appetite for alternative living. Any new project would likely be a magnet rather than a follower in this geography — there is room to be a defining node rather than joining a pre-existing dense network. The immigration-friendly context (see legal.md) means that international collaborators can realistically join within a 1–2 year pathway, which differentiates Nova Scotia from many European comparators.
