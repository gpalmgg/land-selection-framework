# Legal + Economic — Transylvania, Romania

**Headline finding:** Romania's land regime divides sharply between EU/EEA citizens (full freehold purchase since January 2014) and non-EU citizens (no direct freehold purchase of land without a treaty — and Romania has signed no such treaties). The standard non-EU workaround is a Romanian SRL (limited company) which is treated as a Romanian legal person and can buy land. Transylvania remains among Europe's most affordable agricultural-land markets despite a decade of rapid appreciation.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Agricultural arable land cost-per-hectare in Romania 2024: **national average €8,656/ha** (INS Romania 2024 release, published 2025). Transylvania-region averages 2024 (Centru + Nord-Vest regions): roughly **€7,000–€10,000/ha** for general arable; **€4,000–€7,000/ha** for pasture and marginal land; **€12,000–€18,000/ha** for top-quality irrigated parcels in the Mureș basin (INS regional breakdown 2024; PBS Worldwide / NAI Romania commentary 2025). National rises of ~4% in 2024 (current prices) and ~25–40% cumulative over the prior decade. *Vintage caveat: smaller-parcel sales in remote Carpathian villages often transact below INS averages and aren't fully captured in national statistics.*

**Supporting facts:**
- EU/EEA citizens: full freehold ownership of agricultural and forest land since 1 January 2014 (end of accession-treaty transition period).
- Non-EU citizens and entities: **cannot acquire ownership of land** in their own name unless a bilateral treaty exists (Constitution art. 44; Law 312/2005). No such treaty currently exists with any non-EU state — confirmed via DLA Piper REALWORLD and ELRA 2024 guidance.
- Non-EU buyer workaround: incorporate a Romanian SRL (limited-liability company) — capital threshold low (RON 200 / ~€40), Romanian-resident director not required; SRL is a Romanian legal person and may purchase land. Companies controlled by non-EU shareholders are not restricted at corporate level. *This is the standard structure for non-EU regen purchases in Transylvania.*
- Alternative: "superficies" right (drept de superficie) — separates building ownership from land ownership; building can be owned by a non-EU individual on land held by another party. Practical for guesthouses/homestead structures, awkward for large agricultural holdings.
- Pre-emption: Law 17/2014 grants pre-emption rights (in order) to co-owners, lessees, neighbouring landowners, young farmers, Romanian state — agricultural-land sales outside city limits must be advertised at the local primărie for 45 days. Adds 2–4 months to transaction time.
- Anti-speculation tax: sales of agricultural land within 8 years of purchase carry an 80% tax on the gain (Law 17/2014 amended 2020) — meaningful for non-speculative practitioners but worth noting.
- Collective-ownership structures: Asociație (NGO law 26/2000), Cooperativă agricolă (Law 566/2004), Composesorat (traditional commons form in Transylvania for forest/pasture, surviving from Habsburg-era law and revived under Law 1/2000).

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Non-EU practitioners almost always operate through a Romanian SRL. This is well-trodden but introduces ongoing Romanian accounting and tax obligations that should be priced in (typically €1,500–€3,000/yr for a small SRL). The pre-emption process is a real lock — surprises can come from neighbours exercising rights at the last moment. *Composesorate* are a uniquely Transylvanian commons form: hereditary membership rooted in local lineages, generally not open to outsiders. Title clarity in Saxon villages is often poor (waves of emigration left scattered heirs in Germany) — title-investigation is non-trivial.
