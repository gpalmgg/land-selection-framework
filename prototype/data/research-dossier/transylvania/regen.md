# Regenerative Ecosystem — Transylvania, Romania

**Headline finding:** Transylvania holds Europe's largest intact mosaic of high-nature-value grasslands and pre-industrial Saxon village landscapes, sustained by smallholder mowing and seasonal transhumance. It hosts a genuinely thick intentional-community scene — Aurora Foundation, Stânceni-area projects, Saxon-village stewardship by foreigners — alongside one of Europe's deepest indigenous regen practices: working peasant farming that never industrialised.

**Key data point (with vintage):** Global Ecovillage Network and Eurotopia directory entries in Transylvania and adjacent Carpathian counties: **~6–10 active community/regen entries** (ecovillage.org and eurotopia.de directories, 2025). Foundation Conservation Carpathia — single regen-conservation actor — has acquired and is restoring **~27,000 ha** of Carpathian forest by 2024 (FCC annual report 2024).

**Supporting facts:**
- Saxon villages (Viscri, Mălâncrav, Floreni, Crit) — King Charles III is patron of the Mihai Eminescu Trust and owns property in Viscri; he has been a long-running advocate of Transylvanian smallholder farming.
- Foundation Conservation Carpathia (FCC) — large-scale forest rewilding in Făgăraș Mountains; partners with European Nature Trust, ELP, Arcadia Fund.
- ADEPT Foundation — agroecology + smallholder support in Târnava Mare area, 2024–2026 portfolio includes EU LIFE-financed pasture restoration.
- Aurora Foundation, Stânceni (Mureș county) — long-standing intentional-community node with permaculture demonstration land.
- Active 2024–2026 funder/programme list (verified): **PNS 2023–2027** (Romania's CAP Strategic Plan, ~€15.8 bn — eco-schemes and HNV grassland measures), **AFM — Administrația Fondului pentru Mediu** (Romanian Environmental Fund, recurring calls for ecological restoration), **EEA & Norway Grants** (Active Citizens Fund and SME-Green programmes through 2024), **Fondul de Mediu** small-grant lines, **Rewilding Europe** active in the Southern Carpathians, **Endangered Landscapes Programme / Arcadia Fund** via FCC.
- HNV (High Nature Value) grassland Romania classification covers ~5 million ha — much of it in Transylvania — and unlocks specific EU eco-scheme payments for traditional management.

**Practitioner-relevant nuance:**
Transylvania's regen distinction is that the *practice* of working with land is alive at peasant-household level — most grassland is still mown by hand or with small machinery. Newcomer projects that integrate with existing village mowing/transhumance economies tend to last; those that try to import a complete external model struggle. Funding stack is real but heavy on EU CAP measures with Romanian-language paperwork — partnering with an established NGO (ADEPT, FCC, MET) for grant access is the standard route. Note that non-EU buyers cannot purchase land in their own name (see legal.md) — most foreign-led regen work runs through Romanian foundations.
