## CRITICAL

### C1. Hero stat, lede, and section heading still say "four" — site shows ten
**Location:** `index.html:7` (meta description), `:13` (og:description), `:1135` (lede: "four real European regions"), `:1139` (hero stat `<div class="num">4</div>`), `:1181` (`<h2>Four European candidates, briefly named.</h2>`)
**What's wrong:** The data, map markers, region grid, criteria bars, summary table, and match bar all render 10 regions (`match-count">10` / "of 10 regions match", `index.html:1203`). But the hero "Candidate regions" stat reads **4**, the lede says "four real European regions," and the regions-intro heading says "Four European candidates." A visitor sees the number 4 in the hero, then scrolls to 10 cards and a "10 of 10 regions match" bar.
**Why it matters (high-traffic):** This is the first thing a shared-link visitor reads. On a site whose entire credibility pitch is "data-honest" and "source + vintage on every value," a visible internal contradiction in the hero undercuts the trust thesis immediately. It also poisons the OG/Twitter share card (`:13`) — the social preview itself says "four."
**Fix:** Change `4`→`10` at `:1139`; "four real European regions"→"ten real European regions" at `:1135`; "Four European candidates"→"Ten European candidates" at `:1181`; update both meta descriptions at `:7` and `:13`.
**Effort:** S

### C2. Methodology's named "eight criteria" do not match the site's actual eight criteria
**Location:** `deeper.html:416` vs `regions.js` `criteria[]`
**What's wrong:** deeper.html states the eight criteria are: *climate, water, soil and biodiversity, energy autonomy, political stability, regenerative ecosystem, legal and economic context, accessibility and demographics.* But the actual eight criteria rendered on the site (`regions.js`) are: **Climate trajectory, Water stress, Soil organic carbon, Forest cover trajectory, Solar PV potential, Conflict proximity, Regenerative network density, Population density.**
  - "Forest cover trajectory" (askjaNumber 11) and "Population density" (#9) are ON the site but NOT in the methodology's named eight.
  - "Legal and economic context" is in the methodology's named eight (and has a `legal.md` dossier dimension for all 10 regions) but has **no criterion card on the site** — there is no land-cost/legal criterion in the live tool.
  - "Energy autonomy" maps loosely to "Solar PV potential," "political stability" to "Conflict proximity," "accessibility and demographics" to "Population density" — but the names a reader is told to expect don't appear.
**Why it matters:** The deeper page is the credibility layer — the place a skeptical reader goes to check the method. Telling them the spine is one set of eight, then showing a different set of eight (one of which, legal/economic, is entirely absent from the tool despite being heavily sourced in the case studies — Alentejo €8k–20k/ha, Ireland €17,875/acre, Romania €8,656/ha are all in the case-study prose) is a genuine framework-consistency failure, not just wording. A reader can reasonably conclude the "eight criteria" claim is loose.
**Fix:** Reconcile the two lists. Either (a) rewrite `deeper.html:416` to name the actual eight criteria the site shows (including forest trajectory and population, and dropping the standalone "legal and economic" since it isn't a criterion card), or (b) if "legal and economic" is genuinely intended to be a criterion, add it to `regions.js` (the dossier `legal.md` data exists for all 10). Option (a) is the honest, lower-effort path given the data on hand.
**Effort:** M

### H2. Conflict criterion: source version and metric window contradict the data and the dossiers
**Location:** `regions.js:669` (criterion metric `'Fatal political-violence events 2020–2024'`), `regions.js:671` (criterion source `'UCDP GED v25.1'`) — vs every conflict cell (`vintage: '2019–2024'`, `source: 'UCDP GED v24.1'`, e.g. `:144–145`) and the dossiers (every `stability.md` says "2019–2024" and "UCDP GED v24.1").
**What's wrong:** The criterion-card header says the metric covers **2020–2024** and cites **UCDP GED v25.1**. The actual per-region values, the footer-bibliography (`deeper.html:759`), and all ten dossier files say **2019–2024** and **v24.1**. So the criterion card footer (which renders `crit.source` = "UCDP GED v25.1") will display a different dataset version than the per-value "Sources for conflict proximity (10 regions)" expander beneath it (which renders each cell's `v.source` = "UCDP GED v24.1"). Same card, two different cited versions of the same dataset.
**Why it matters:** This is a self-contradiction visible *within a single card* on a site whose headline promise is source+vintage rigor. v25.1 is also a forward-looking version string not corroborated anywhere else in the codebase — it looks like an un-synced edit.
**Fix:** In `regions.js`, set criterion `conflict` source to `'UCDP GED v24.1'` and metric to `'Fatal political-violence events 2019–2024'` to match the data and dossiers. (Or, if v25.1 is genuinely the intended dataset, update all 10 cells + 10 dossiers + the bibliography — far larger; the data/dossier "v24.1 / 2019–2024" is the corroborated truth, so the criterion header is the outlier to fix.)
**Effort:** S

### H3. Solar PV vintage label is internally inconsistent (data vs deeper.html vs sources)
**Location:** All 10 solar cells in `regions.js` (`vintage: '1999–2018 avg'`, e.g. `:139`); `deeper.html:466/475/504/533` (case-study prose says "Global Solar Atlas 2020" / "Global Solar Atlas, 2020"); `deeper.html:737` bibliography ("Global Solar Atlas — Solargis / World Bank (2020 release)").
**What's wrong:** The data file dates the solar values "1999–2018 avg." The deeper page repeatedly dates the same source "2020." The Global Solar Atlas 2020 release is indeed built on a 1994–2018 (or 1999–2018) long-term average, so both can be technically true — but the site presents two different vintage strings for the same numbers without reconciling them, which a careful reader will flag.
**Why it matters:** Lower-stakes than H2, but it's the same class of issue (vintage discipline is the site's core claim) and it's visible: the criterion card shows "1999–2018 avg," the case study says "2020."
**Fix:** Pick one convention. Recommend the data carry `vintage: '2020 release (1999–2018 LTA)'` or align deeper.html prose to "1999–2018 average (GSA 2020 release)." Cheapest: leave data, add "(2020 release)" once.
**Effort:** S

## MEDIUM

### M1. Climate criterion: per-region sources and scenario labels are heterogeneous but the criterion claims one source/scenario
**Location:** `regions.js` climate cells — most are `WorldClim CMIP6 v2.1` / `SSP2-4.5`, BUT connemara (`:255`) uses `EPA Research 471 (Met Éireann TRANSLATE)`, pembrokeshire (`:302`) uses `Met Office UKCP18`, cevennes (`:347`) uses `Météo-France DRIAS` with vintage **`2041–2060 RCP4.5`** (not SSP2-4.5). The criterion definition (`regions.js:586`, and `index.html` card footer) declares the source as "WorldClim CMIP6 (SSP2-4.5)" for the whole card.
**What's wrong:** Three of ten regions use a different climate source than the card's stated source, and one (Cévennes) uses a different emissions scenario (RCP4.5 vs SSP2-4.5) — mixing CMIP5-era and CMIP6-era pathways in a single comparison column without flagging it. The per-value source expander does surface the real per-region source (good — the UI is honest at cell level), so this is a "card header overstates uniformity" issue, not hidden data.
**Why it matters:** Comparing an RCP4.5 number against SSP2-4.5 numbers in the same temperature column is a real methodological wrinkle for a 50–100-year siting decision. It's defensible (national downscaled products are often better than WorldClim at this scale) but should be acknowledged, not papered over by a single-source card header.
**Fix:** Either add a one-line note on the climate card ("regional downscaled products substituted where superior; see per-value sources") or, for Cévennes, find an SSP2-4.5 equivalent. At minimum normalize the Cévennes vintage scenario language.
**Effort:** M

### M2. Population criterion metric says "projected 2030" but all values are observed census vintages 2021–2024
**Location:** `regions.js:703` (criterion metric `'Persons per km² (projected 2030)'` and source `'JRC GHSL POP R2023A'`) vs the 10 population cells whose vintages are `2021/2022/2023/2024` and whose sources are national statistics offices (INE, CSO, INS, ONS, INSEE, ASTAT, SADEI, Statistisches Landesamt, Statistikaamet, IGE — e.g. `:154, :200, :245`).
**What's wrong:** The criterion header claims projected-2030 GHSL data; the actual values are recent observed census densities from national agencies. Two different things (a 2030 projection vs a 2021–2024 observation) and two different sources (GHSL vs national stats). The card footer will cite "JRC GHSL POP R2023A" while the per-value expander cites national censuses.
**Why it matters:** Same source+vintage discipline issue as H2/H3. "Projected 2030" is also a stronger claim than the data supports (these are observed, not projected).
**Fix:** Change criterion metric to `'Persons per km² (recent census)'` and source to reflect that values are national-statistics-office census figures (or keep GHSL as the criterion's *intended* canonical source but note values are currently national-census stand-ins). The footer (`index.html:1285`) lists "JRC GHSL" as a cited source — if no value actually uses GHSL, that footer credit is also unearned.
**Effort:** M

## FRAMEWORK-DISCIPLINE CHECK (the non-negotiables)

**PASS.** No violations found.
- **No composite scoring / weighting / ranking:** Confirmed. `main.js` has no sort, no score aggregation, no weighting. The only `score`/`weight` tokens are MapLibre paint expressions (`:293, :314, :334` — `['get','score']` reads the water-stress geojson field; `heatmap-weight` is a render property) — not framework scoring. `regionPasses()` (`:76`) is pure boolean threshold AND-ing; the match count (`:611`) counts booleans, it does not rank.
- **Threshold sliders filter, never score:** Confirmed. Sliders set `state.thresholds`; regions pass/fail per criterion independently; no aggregation into a number-per-place.
- **Native units throughout:** Confirmed. °C, score, g/kg, %/decade, kWh/kWp, events, sites, p/km² — no universal normalized grid in the data (normalize() is used only for bar-fill WIDTH and ramp color, i.e. display geometry, never for comparison/scoring).
- **State + trajectory:** Partially by-design. There are NO explicit per-cell `state`/`trajectory` fields (grep: 0). Trajectory is encoded into the value+label for the two trajectory-style criteria (climate = mid-century projection; forest = %/decade trend) and into `criteria[].framing`. This matches the CLAUDE.md framing ("State + trajectory in one number"), so it's an intentional design choice, not a gap — but note the data model does not structurally separate the two, so a future "show current vs projected" feature has no field to bind to.
- **Source + vintage + license visible in UI:** Confirmed. Per-value source+vintage render in the per-criterion `<details>` expander (`index.html:1421–1464`); criterion-level source+nativeUnit+license render in each card footer (`main.js:526–530`). License is shown at criterion level only (not per-cell), which is consistent with how licenses attach to datasets.

The match bar's footer line "Nothing here is composite, weighted, or scored across criteria" (`index.html:1285`) is accurate.
