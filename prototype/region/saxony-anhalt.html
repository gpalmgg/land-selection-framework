A place, read closely

Saxony-Anhalt rural

Germany

Chernozem plain plus Harz foothills. Sieben Linden (founded 1997) and ZEGG nearby anchor Germany’s strongest ecovillage cluster. Higher water stress than Atlantic peers; mature regulatory environment.

What living here asks of you

Saxony-Anhalt asks you to prove you intend to farm before it will sell you land. Germany sets no nationality bar, but the Grundstücksverkehrsgesetz and the Landgesellschaft give active farmers pre-emption priority, so community projects without an agricultural Nutzungskonzept routinely get blocked at the Landwirtschaftsbehörde, and the standard route is a German agricultural Genossenschaft or GmbH with a farmer-shareholder. Physically, this is one of Germany's driest regions, the Mitteldeutsches Trockengebiet, where you must design for 6 to 9 month dry-summer bridges and real winter frost at once. The hardest part is social: rural Saxony-Anhalt has lost population continuously since 1990, AfD strength shapes local dynamics, and you will need German-language capacity and years of local-economic relationship-building before newcomers are trusted.

Source: Grundstücksverkehrsgesetz (GrdstVG)

The first gate, legal and cost

Across the slate the data shows these two as the decisive constraints, more often than soil, climate or water. They sit before everything else.

Legal & ownership
Foreign ownership
yes Germany imposes no nationality restriction on freehold land ownership, but agricultural-land purchases over ~1 ha require Landwirtschaftsbehorde approval under GrdstVG regardless of nationality.
Collective ownership path
Agricultural eingetragene Genossenschaft or GmbH with a farmer-shareholder (plus written Nutzungskonzept) is the standard vehicle; GbR, Stiftung, and e.V. are also available for community functions
Multi-household residence as-of-right
no
Planning gate for living
Bauordnung permit under Baugesetzbuch sec. 35 (Aussenbereich), normally via the §35 privilegierte Vorhaben farm-related route
Pre-emption / first-claim holders
active farmers via the GrdstVG / Landwirtschaftsbehorde; Landgesellschaft Sachsen-Anhalt
Key restriction
The Grundstucksverkehrsgesetz lets the Landwirtschaftsbehorde refuse purchases by non-active farmers if a farmer will buy at the same price, so pure-investor or non-agricultural community purchases are routinely blocked.
Regulatory direction
stable BVVG is in wind-down per the 2018 Bundestag decision; remaining ex-GDR state-land sales continue through 2024 to 2030.

Source: Grundstucksverkehrsgesetz (GrdstVG)
